That's a whole lot of IF's, Michael, but IF all those things were to come together, it 
certainly would be a quandary as to whether or not approaching a complete stranger 
would be appropriate.  One can argue that the person in spirit has gone to great lengths 
to make contact with their loved one.  However.......

On the flip side, just because someone is in spirit, does not mean that they are 
automatically omniscient, nor that they have the right to impose their will (or you impose 
your will, for that matter) on a loved one who may not be open to the communication 
emotionally or spiritually.  

Assuming we are (as many indications have it) pretty much the same person in spirit that 
we were here in the physical (our personalities remain fairly in tact, though just as here, 
there can be growth and change on the other side), it would not be out of the realm of 
possibility for them to tell you their loved one is open to communication, when in fact they 
might not be.

I guess my bottom line is this:  There are so many people out there who are open to the 
idea of EVP communication and are seeking to make contact with their departed loved 
ones, that there is no need for approaching strangers with messages.  The potential for 
harm is just too great, and it is not our place (or the place of the person in spirit) to make 
these kind of decisions for others.  If this is what interests you (helping those in spirit to 
pass messages along), then seek to make connections with people in the physical who are 
openly and willingly on board your assistance.  

Margaret
