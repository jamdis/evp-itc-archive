Oh, OK.

If you want to use a common ground for the power supply, you must put a (+)
voltage on pin 4 and the same (-) voltage on pin 11. For example, +12v on
pin 4 and -12v on pin 11. The input and output will show zero DC volts with
no input, referenced to the power supply grounds.

If you put a (+) voltage on pin 4 and ground pin 11, the input and output
pins will reference to half that voltage. So if you put +12v on pin 4 and
ground pin 11 with a single power supply voltage, then the output will have
6 volts on it and anything referenced to "ground" on a schematic must
actually be referenced to 6 volts. You would have to use DC blocking
capacitors on the input and output to connect to other circuits.
