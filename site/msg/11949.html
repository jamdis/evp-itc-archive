The topic of Spiritualism came up, and I think it's an important
topic. I'm a Spiritualist. As a matter of fact, I will be in Lily
Dale, NY (The Village of Spirits) at the end of Sept. for a workshop
on Mediumship. Anyone want to join me? Come on Misty! Wouldn't we have
a blast? What a place to record.

Anyway, I mention Spiritualism as it's practice has helped me in my
research and due to the non-dogmatic structure, leaves my mind open to
ponder without setting boundaries or labels on spirit. Spiritualism
overseas is fabulous. Especially in the UK & Ireland. The talk of such
things is much more accepted and how I love that. No silly
superstitious tags and spirit is greatly respected and paid attention
to.

My dealings with Findhorn (in Scotland) have helped immensely in my
understanding of the Elemental and Devic energies I've often been told
I'm recording. Am I positive I'm recording these certain energies
called Devas, Faeries, and Elementals? Nope. That's just what they
tell me. I'm told all sorts of things and always take them in stride.

Do I combine my Spiritualism with my EVP/ITC research? Nope. Not
always, but sometimes. I like solid evidence that can be analyzed and
scientific evidence. I like to be able to record something I can hand
over on a brand new audio tape and have it analyzed or ask what the
chances are that stray radio signals are calling out my name and
answering my questions in real time. Or simply answer them with no
radio at all - just a faucet trickling or no sound whatsoever.

What about others here? Do you combine your beliefs with your
recording or stay completely scientific?

Wisteria
