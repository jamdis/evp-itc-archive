Hi Bruce, and Jeremy too!  Essentially, we are in agreement and share a similar moral 
philosophy, despite my posts defending the rights of others to take advantage of the 
market (even if I don't like it).  ;-)

My main concern, and difference of opinion, is with your assertion, Bruce, that these units 
(sold by price gouging people - no disagreement there) will come with the "wrong energy 
attached to them."  I'm perfectly happy to agree to disagree on this aspect of the topic at 
hand, and I think others are perfectly capable of making up their own minds on what they 
think, now that different points of view are on the table for consideration.

There are some people who are very black and white when it comes to the topic of making 
money in the paranormal field.  This is why I posed the questions to you both about Ron 
and shared the story of my friend.  I am not one of the people in the black and white 
camp.  Jeremy, it appears that you are, and you are not alone, that's for sure.  I believe in 
people's right to profit in the field, but I absolutely wish to see it be done in a fair and 
honorable manner.  That being said, I also have respect for those in the black and white 
camp, and I can even understand where you are coming from.

I don't know Ron personally, but I can tell you in another forum when the erroneous 
assumption was made that he was price gouging, I similarly stated my views about his 
right to profit from the sale of a quality product, which performs as stated.  I understand 
it's not much profit, as he does specify on his site that he sells these units for "close to 
actual cost" (so Bruce, I'm not under the same impression he is selling at a loss.)  As well, 
he states that the soon to be released MaxiBox will be intended for the experienced users, 
and the MiniBox will remain in production as the standard system, implying it is an entry 
level unit.  Hopefully, as is often the case, production costs can eventually be reduced.

Similarly, I do not know Chris.  If he is, as I've read others claim - "less than" he presents 
himself to be, and falls short in terms of services offered - than certainly his practices 
would fall somewhere near, or even well within, the realm of charlatanism and I have 
already expressed my distain for this type of behavior.  

My closing wish is this:  May those who are interested in obtaining a commercially 
available 'ghost box' (of whatever flavor), be blessed with one coming into their lives, 
either by gift or by purchasing one at a fair price.  

Thanks for the discussion, guys.  I appreciate it.  

Margaret
