The recording is the easy part, they do all the work coming through. I
have a digital recorder as well and I simply use EVPmaker as my ears
have become accustomed to listening to it.

Steps that I use.
1)About 20 minutes before I let those that want to talk with me know I
am going to be recording.
2)Hook up an omni-directional mic to the recorder
3)Bring up EVPmaker and load file leaving it at the default settings
4)Set my volume to full wav and 45% main
5)Record for approximately 2 minutes playing the EVPM file
6)Hook the recorder to the PC using a USB cable
7)Copy and label the file moving onto my PC
8)I lower the HZ from the default in Audacity(free program)to 10000hz.
This was a suggestion by those on the other side to help me hear the
file better.
9)Start to trim off small chunks of the file and listen, slowing the
tempo by 10% after each pass and then speeding it up by 10% when it
gets often times distorted because I went too low.
10)Label and save the selected section and move on
11)Let the spirits know what I heard, so this lets them know what I
didn't so if they feel something was important they can try to let me
know what I missed on the next recording.
