> Thought I'd jump in here...
>
> First of all - I use mainly full spectrum sound recorders rather than
> the standard hand-held digital voice recorders. Two reasons: 1. There
> are paranormal NON-VOICE sounds out there that are not being picked
> up by the limited response of the voice-only recorders, 2. The higher
> frequencies in the 20-20 kHz human sound range contribute to the
> intelligibility of consonants. Using recorders with a higher
> frequency response makes for clearer understanding of voices -
> particularly those that are "whispered".
>
> My first foray into using full spectrum recorders led me to buy the
> (now discontinued) Fostex MR-8.
>
> http://namm.harmony-central.com/WNAMM03/Content/Fostex/PR/MR-8.html
>
> While it is not extremely portable nor hand-held, it is "luggable"
> and is perfect for stationary recording. It is full spectrum, battery-
> powered, uses Compact Flash and has inputs for multiple external
> microphones. [The later models use hard drives for storage making
> them less rugged for field use, IMHO, and require AC power making
> them less flexible as to location.] You can find used units in good
> condition and relatively cheap on ebay. (Oh - and you can use either
> USB download or read directly off the CF for storage/analysis.) Plus
> the multiple inputs allow you to record infrsound and ultrasound on
> separate channels in synch with the human audible sound range.
>
> For over a year now - I've used the Zoom H4.
>
> http://www.samsontech.com/products/productpage.cfm?
> prodID=1901&brandID=4
>
> http://www.zzounds.com/item--ZOMH4
>
> Again...full spectrum, battery-operated, hand-held, uses SD storage.
> USB interface. The built-in stereo mikes are amazingly sensitive but
> the unit also has inputs for external mikes along with phantom power.
> I use it with a pair of noise-cancelling headphones and have
> definitly heard EVPs in real-time.
>
> Zoom also has a newer model - the H2. Just as good.
>
> http://www.samsontech.com/products/productpage.cfm?
> prodID=1916&brandID=4
>
> http://www.zzounds.com/item--ZOMH2
>
> The only complaint I have about the H4 is that it doesn't have a
> lanyard (I'd hate to drop it) nor does it come with a carrying case
> (I bought a new case for a Fluke multimeter at an electronics supply
> shop which does the trick).
