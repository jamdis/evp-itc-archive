Hi Lyn.  I've listened to all but your longest file.  It would be really helpful if you snipped 
out and isolated, just the area where you suspect an EVP, because they have a lot of 
irrelevant material.  I can only speak for myself, but I have a fairly short attention span 
when it comes to listening to other's clips (because I listen to so many).  I want to focus in 
right away on the relevant areas without having to work my way there.  ;0)

I have a couple suggestions for you based on what I did listen to.  First, take your recorder 
off of voice activation.  Depending on the model, it's quite likely that you may miss an EVP 
because it can get cutoff (as your mom was telling you, there is - in some models - a 
slight delay from the time the VOX kicks in and when it actually records.  In some cases, 
the EVP may cause it to kick on, but won't get recorded due to the delay).  VOX is a great 
feature to use when you are going to leave a recorder unattended in a room for a long 
period of time (a practice I'm not fond of, but I know some others like to do this).  

Next, you have to talk much less than it seems you are.  It doesn't leave them any time to 
answer. (though I realize if VOX was on, it's possible you were being quiet and nothing 
was happening, just making it seem like you didn't wait between questions/comments) 
Ask a question, then wait silently for period of time to give them a chance to answer.  I like 
to wait about 10-15 seconds, but I know some people like to wait 30 seconds.  You might 
even make a list the things you want to ask, and go off of that, so your session stays on 
track.  

Also, set the recorder down on something.  This way you won't create any noises by 
holding it.  And make note of things in the room such as the ticking clock which runs 
throughout the recordings.  This can also wreak havoc with your VOX, so ignore the 
blinking light as being any kind of indicator that paranormal something is happening.

I don't know what other kinds of questions you have, but if you toss them out here, maybe 
someone (or several someones) here can give you their take.  

In the last clip, you are moving the recorder (assuming), as well as talking, moving in a 
squeaking chair, and again the clock ticking... those are all things that will trigger the 
VOX.  If you listen to it, you will hear that it did indeed record you typing on the keyboard 
and all your conversation regarding the blinking light, etc.  I'm  not hearing anything that 
seem worrisome, or even paranormal.  Maybe I'm missing something that someone else 
will catch when they listen.  

Keep trying!  Sometimes it takes a while.
