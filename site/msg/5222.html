There are several application for voice recognition (VR). Most cel
phones can be trained to recognize voice prints from your spoken word
which creates a pattern. Microsoft's Vista OS has voice recognition
included for free so basic functions (open file, view, play, save, and
so on) can be done with a mic and your voice. It can be integrated
into Word so you can speak and a letter can be composed. But this type
of VR just uses a stored voice pattern and can adapted to identify the
 person who was recorded assuming there is a known sample of the
persons voice that can be referenced. VR software of this type is
usually priced between $100 to $200 if it's not included for free with
another software bundle.

Another type of VR is used for voice stress analysis. One of the more
popular ones is Truster. You can buy this as software or as portable
hardware piece that has an audio in jack. It measures the inflections
that usually indicate the stress of attempted deception. It goes for
around $95.

In evp's it's more a matter of isolating the frequency of the sound as
by their very nature evp's are inaudible. The audio sampling software
I have seen allow you to easily display the frequency range. The
voiced speech of a typical adult male with have an average frequency
of from 85 to 155 Hz, and that of a typical adult female from 165 to
255 Hz. Humans can hear sounds at frequencies between 20Hz to
20,000Hz. Evp's are usually in the very low range, below what most of
can hear even if they are above 20Hz.

For Box recordings, we'll have to be creative. With the rapid audio
shifts it would be a challenge but I like the idea of further
analyzing the recordings. -Bill
