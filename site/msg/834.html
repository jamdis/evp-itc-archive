> The Intelligence Project

> A Project involving EVP and Listening

> If you have done EVP for a while you will already know whether or

> You may believe this to be so, and so may I, but the world in

> our "deteriorating mental ability"!

> You can't prove anything - all you can do is state your firm 
belief 
> and perhaps back that up with some examples which you hope don't 
> sound too "made-up".
> 
> This project aims to deal with that situation once and for all.  
We 
> believe in working with science rather than against it.  Science 
is 
> not our enemy, nor indeed is scepticism.
> 
> Method
> What we want to find out is if there is any relationship between 
> what the experimenter says and the type of EVP responses that we 
get.
> 
> We are looking for signs of intelligence.
> 
> We want to do that in a measurable way - for that is the way of 
> science.  And this is the way that has been approved by a major 
> European pharmaceutical company for funding.
> 
> We have six questions. These questions are decided on in advance.  
> 
> During the experiment the experimenter asks each of the questions, 
> in turn, with a 30-second gap after each question to allow any 
> responses to be recorded. 
> 
> So (for example) what you would get is,
> 
> Question 'What is the time?' 
> [30 seconds delay]; 
> Question 'What is your name?' 
> [30 seconds delay], 
> Question 'What do you like best?'
> [30 second delay]
> and so on.
> 
> Now if an utterance is heard within the 30-second period and if 
the 
> utterance sounds as though it could be a response to the question, 
> then it is treated as an actual response.  For example if we hear 
> the utterance 'John Smith' seven seconds after the question 'What 
is 
> your name?' is asked, then that looks like a response to that 
> question.
> 
> The experimenter carries out the session, asking each of the 
> questions in turn; and then the recording of the session is turned 
> over to a speech analyst.
> 
> The analyst picks out from the general background noise all the 
> utterances he can find in the recording.  He refines his search 
> still further by selecting out the 16 utterances of the best 
> quality. (sometimes it will be less than 16), and "shuffles" these 
> so that you will not know which came first, which came second and 
so 
> on.
> 
> The final 16 utterances, their order shuffled, are sent out to you.
> 
> What you are asked to do is decide if any of the utterances could 
be 
> a response to any of the questions, and to fill in a table like 
that 
> shown in the example below.
> 
> Track No.	Could be a response to Question Number..
> BR01	                               4
> BR02	                               0
> BR03	                               0
> BRO4	                               3
> BR05	                               1
> BR06	                               2
> BR07	                               3
> BRO8	                               3
> BR09	                               6
> BR10	                               0
> BR11	                               0
> BR12	                               0
> BR13	                               6
> BR14	                               5
> BR15	                               0
> BR16	                               0
>  
> As you can see some utterances are not considered to be a response 
> to any of the questions, and so a '0' is inserted at that point.  
> This is not an easy test, that is why you are being asked to help.
