> Hi Nathan. I always look to relevance as a key in determining 
whether or not something is appropriate to the session. If this were 
a hoax, I would then have to seek an explanation for how private 
information is known by the 'hoax perp', particularly information 
that would require visual awareness. Some of us have examples of 
appropriate responses being given when asking things like, "Can you 
name something nearby me?" or "Please tell me what I'm holding in my 
left hand." A sophisticated multi-transmission two way radio would 
not explain answers which require 'visual' information of some kind 
(though I'll be the first to admit my understanding of electronics is 
limited but I'm fairly confident on this aspect.) Of course, there is 
always the chance that the person running the hoax is also, or in 
partnership with, an extremely talented remote viewer or psychic who 
is able to glean that visual information from the ether or the mind 
of the experimenter. And what of those examples where people have 
confirmed the sound of the voice being "just like" that of their 
loved one in spirit? Surely the hoaxer isn't also able to mimic 
people's loved ones.If you are using EVP Maker with anything other 
than the allophones file, which contains no words to begin with, you 
are also running the possibility of 'false positives' albeit they 
wouldn't be from a hoax. Again, I look to relevance to be key, and 
relevant info that would not come easily to a stranger is even 
better. For example, I turned on my MiniBox for the very first time 
and said, "Hello?" and immediately received the response, "Hello 
Margaret." If this were a hoax, how could that person know in less 
than 10 seconds, who I was by name. I had not given this information 
out. Not to mention that you have people experimenting 24/7 and 
receiving similar responses. How in the world could an organization 
have the manpower, technology, or even the desire, to attempt such a 
hoax? I mean that as a genuine inquiry, because there might be an 
explanation I have not thought of. I have one example where, via 
radio sweep, my communicator was able to correctly identify the 
status and location of a missing person (who it turns out was alive 
and well, so we ended up with confirmation that the info given had 
indeed been correct - which is much less complicated that trying to 
track and understand the clues when associated with the dead, or 
missing but not found.) Again, if a hoax, would that have simply been 
a lucky guess on the part of the hoaxer?Bottom line for me is ... 
have I wondered if it could be a hoax? Or simply a fortuitous tuning 
of the dial? Yes, on both accounts. But there is enough compelling 
evidence to rule out both of them as an explanation for the far too 
many relevant examples received by so many of us.Good food for 
thought, but after digesting it, I'd have to say, "No, a 
sophisticated hoax does not sufficiently explain the phenomena on a
