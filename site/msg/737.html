Hello, Lyn.

Have you ever read anything on poltergeists?

One of the most common signs of a poltergeist "visit" (if you will) 
is the movement of objects. Less common but still a sign of a 
possible poltergeist is a disembodied voice or voices.

Now - I'm not saying you have a poltergeist "visitation" (in fact - 
another common sign is that the phenomenon is short-lived...a few 
months...not an extended length of time like yours) but if you do - 
you'll find that the phenomenon takes on the personality of a 
trickster/practical joker. Due to this "personality" - it becomes 
difficult, even impossible to "catch" anything using sound recorders, 
still cameras, video, etc. As if "it" or "they" don't want evidence 
of "their" existence just to frustrate/annoy the people 
being "visited".

One of the most popular theories concerning poltergeists is 
that "they" are not really spirits but the outward manifestation of 
frustration/anger/confusion of a real person (possibly even more than 
one) in the house...known as the "agent". The agent is creating the 
phenomena subconsciously using psychokinesis (commonly called "mind 
over matter") so the theory is called RSPK for Recurrent Spontaneous 
PsychoKinesis.

Do you have at least one adolescent in the house who is undergoing 
puberty?

One of the most famous poltergeist cases involving nearly every known 
sign of poltergeist activity such as moving objects AND voices was 
the Enfield Poltergeist case back in the late '70s. You might be 
fascinated to read up on the case at this web site:

http://www.zurichmansion.org/ghosts/poltergeist.htm

In the meantime - as long as no one gets hurt and your household is 
not totally disrupted by this...I'd say just accept it and go on with 
your lives.

Hope that helps you somewhat.

Guy
