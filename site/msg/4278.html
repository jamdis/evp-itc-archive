Hi LC,

Yes, the "click" can be cut down a bit by using a chamber.  It's 
a "phase" thing.  When the "click" (or any sound wave) is reached at 
the input mic at 180 degrees out of phase with the original I 
understand there is a cancel effect.  That works with any sound wave I 
think, so you're bound to have some "clicks" and their 180 out of phase 
wave reaching the mic nearly at the same time, cancelling somewhat.
There is so much bouncing going on in there I would love to see what 
the entire pattern looks lke on a scope.  There must be alot of phase 
shifting going on.

I tried my prototype with and without the chamber yesterday.  I found a 
BIG different, and I'm sticking with the chamber effect.  For one 
thing, it increased the amplification at least three-fold when I used 
the chamber, not to mention all the mixing and phasing.

show. Both from my chamber system and from the three 12-469/mixer 
system.
