--0-535318496-1278860479=:99047 Content-Type: text/plain; charset=us-ascii There is a good way to verify a file irrespective of its contents without revealing the contents. This technique is used in the software distribution business to ensure files have not been contaminated prior to or during a download. What you do is make a 'digital hash' of the files contents. This renders a long hexadecimal number which is reasonably unique and will change if the file or file contents are changed. The experimenter can then take a picture for example, create the MD5 hash and send that hash out as proof of the file's contents, without revealing the picture content in this case. Later, once the picture is posted, anyone can run a similar hashing program against the file to confirm they have the same file whose hash was posted by the experimenter originally. Hence you know what you see is a valid copy of the original (picture in this case). The software is available free on the Internet. One example of many may be found at: http://www.fileformat.info/tool/hash.htm You don't even have to download it. Just click the browse button to open a file on your computer and the program displays a large variety of hashes including the MD5 hash.
