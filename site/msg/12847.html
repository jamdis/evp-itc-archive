Hi Al,
In the USA, the AM Band channel spacing is 10kHz, so 1485kHz isnt 
allowed to be in use, but the channels either side are, meaning 1480 
and 1490 are the nearest channels in use. Ive found reference to an 
FFC listing of some stations in the USA for 1480 and 1490 (there are 
probably more, and/or this list may be out of date or incorrect):
1480 WZRC  NEW YORK        NY
1480 WDAS  PHILADELPHIA    PA   
1480 WEEO  SHIPPENSBURG    PA   
1490 WAZL  HAZLETON        PA   
1490 WBCB  LEVITTOWN       PA   
1490 WUSS  PLEASANTVILLE   NJ   
1490 WLPA  LANCASTER       PA   
1490 WARK  HAGERSTOWN      MD   
1490 WNTJ  JOHNSTOWN       PA   

It is important to realise that stations on 1480 or 1490 can possibly 
interfere with any intended reception on the unused frequency of 1485 
being only half a channel away.

In Europe and here in NZ, the channel spacing is 9kHz, so there are 
stations using the Jurgenson frequency of 1485kHz
In a quick Google search, I see there are Polish, German and Latvian 
stations listed for this frequency.
