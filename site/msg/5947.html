Bruce--I do think perhaps there is one point here that possibly  
should be considered. Hear me out, okay, my friend? I do think that  
maybe I will start attaching original and cleaned up samples  
together, so the two can be compared. We both know that we clean them  
up to help the message, which *is* there and not added by us, to be  
better heard. That's not the argument--at least not from me. But it  
might be helpful, in general, to hear the two versions side by side.

This said, to Babs: You're going to have to live with the fact that  
not all of the communication is in response to questions. If a  
question was spoken near where the communication came through, I try  
to include it. But there are plenty of times when I am not being  
addressed at all, as best I can tell. They're talking amonst  
themselves and issuing orders, etc. So, clips without questions  
aren't going to change and you need to accept them for what they are.

As far as removing time: I don't see an issue with it if the clip is  
noted, rather as in journalism where we add ... to denote that  
material has been removed that was tangential to the main thrust of  
the quote.

Personally, I have to slow my communication stuff down by about half.  
It comes through incredibly fast. I don't think I'm alone. Another  
deal-with-it issue. Slowing is not manipulating. Neither is  
amplification of fainter responses or deamplification of  
nonassociated noise.

Some if us are recording mono and some stereo. Silencing one track to  
better hear another is also not manipulating it falls in the  
journalistic convention "..." category, to me anyway.

Still, point taken on combining both unprocessed and processed clips.

As for all the rest of the fretting about counting stations and  
dividing up times and all, I'd say if you want to do it, knock  
yourself out. I don't think it ultimately matters because we're  
already postulating that most of the time, the voice we hear is not  
the real voice of the communicant. It doesn't matter if you hear  
blips of the same announcer coming through at 15 second intervals.  
It's what the voice might say that is important. I know that isn't  
what you want to hear, but I think it's probably valid.

Ann
