Aluminum wire is a nightmare! The stuff tends to overheat, oxidizes where
interconnections are made and will spark, etc.

I bought a transformer at Gateway Electronics in St. Louis some years ago
that looked like a great transformer for a high-current 12 volt supply. The
secondary appeared to be wound with #8 enameled wire. I found out the
secondary was wound with aluminum wire. After I built the power supply and
tried to get 20 amps from it but couldn't, I gave up on it and got another
transformer wound with copper wire.
Zack
