When I first listened to the Spiricom samples several years ago, my immediate impression was: Hmm, it sounds like someone using an "electronic larynx" (speech aid device). This impression was strengthened by the observation that the buzzing sound often was even modulated by O'Neill's own voice during the dialoges between him and "Dr. Mueller"! (Did O'Neill forget to remove the device from his throat while talking to his counterpart?)

And what about the "13 frequencies" that are supposed to be used as the "carrier" for the spirit voices? Why aren't they audible in the recordings? And why does a mixture of the 13 tones created with Audition sound so different from the typical "Spiricom buzz"? - Well, if you analyze the frequency spectrum of the buzzing sound (this can be done with Audition etc.), you will find a lot of harmonics (peaks in the spectrum), and some of these harmonics more or less match the 13 Spiricom frequencies...

To me, the entire Spiricom saga produces more questions than answers...
