Let me give you my comments, none of which are meant to belittle your experience, rather to provide food for thought: I am sure that the feelings you experienced ("I began to feel what I can only describe as "thick" energy move into my hand, up my arm, through my chest and then settle into my stomach...It began to churn and move up into my throat..I felt paralyzed. ...It took approximately 30 minutes before I felt myself again,") were very real to you. You said you thought it was evil; that your vomit would be black (an image straight out of classic demonolgy). Indeed, you said that everyone in the room had the similar mindset: Whatever you there to confront was dark/negative/evil.

Speaking from my own personal experience, I have seen groups of people involved in the paranormal convince themselves of similar (and many far, far weirder) things. Hell, as a teen, I was involved in several of these folies-a-deux (or 10, 15, 20, or more). I saw people having experiences similar to what you report. At one point, friends of mine were receiving alledged telepathic communication from a living rock star who was helping them commune with spirit of Alisdar Crowley, for example. Others in the group or on its periphery were getting "attacked" by what we called AWOLs (from "Away Without Leave"). Hell, I thought I'd been attacked by a few myself. But now, many years later, what I see this particular group, and others, was comprised of was a lot of people damned busy scaring the shit out of themselves. Maybe real dark forces *were* feeding off the bad vibes--I dunno. But what I witnessed is rendolent of the Salem hysteria in the late 1600s, the "Amityville Horror" case, and many, many others, in descending order of sensationalism, down to the ubiquitous freakouts of kids playing with the Ouija board.

Fear and anxiety produce painful and uncomfortabl physical effects in the body that could be interpreted as caused by a negative outside influence. Fear causes the body to become flooded with hormones (particularly adrenalin). Here is a list of symptoms (from Wikipedia) of what we commonly call a panic attack:

Physical

    * An extremely unpleasant sensation of adrenaline over the entire body
    * Sweating
    * Shortness of breath
    * Racing or pounding heartbeat or palpitations
    * Chest pain
    * Dizziness or vertigo
    * Lightheadedness
    * Nausea / stomach pains
    * Hyperventilation
    * Choking or smothering sensations
    * Uncontrollable itching
    * Tingling or numbness in the hands, face, feet or mouth (paresthesia)
    * Hot/cold flashes
    * Trembling or shaking
    * Feeling of claustrophobia
    * Exhaustion
    * Feeling of physical weakness or limpness of the body
    * Uncontrollable crying

Mental
    * Loss of the ability to react logically to stimuli
    * Loss of cognitive ability in general
    * Racing thoughts (often based on fear; a repeated or illogical worry)
    * Loud internal dialogue
    * Feeling of impending doom
    * Feeling of "going crazy"
    * Extreme worried feeling
    * Feeling of extreme nervousness
    * Feeling out of control
    * Feeling of Threatening
    * Feeling of anti-social behaviour from other people
    * Feeling of excitement
    * Feeling of nagging from other people
    * Vision is somewhat impaired (eyes may feel like they are shaking.)

Emotional

    * Terror, or a sense that something unimaginably horrible is about to occur and one is powerless to prevent it
    * Fear that the panic is a symptom of a serious illness
    * Fear of losing control
    * Fear of death
    * Fear of going crazy
    * Flashbacks to earlier panic trigger

 Perceptual

    * Tunnel vision
    * Heightened senses
    * The apparent slowing down or speeding up of time
    * Dream-like sensation or perceptual distortion (derealization)
    * Dissociation, or the perception that one is not connected to the body or is disconnected from space and time (depersonalization)
    * Feeling of loss of free will, as if acting entirely automatically without control

I have to submit that the symptoms you describe for yourself and others involved that night are found on this list. In fact, a lot of descriptions of "attacks by dark forces" sound like the physiological effects of fear and anxiety. Am I saying you had a panic attack? No. But I am suggesting it is a possibility to consider. Because you were in a situation where you believed that "evil" would be present, thus generating fear, Occam's Razor would certainly lead us to conclude that adrenaline that made you feel the way you did. But Occam's Razor proves it's general trustworthines by periodic exceptions.

HMO and not an attack or judgement on your (or anyone's) beliefs,

Ann
