Hi Bruce.  The same action can be taken (sharing the information about how to hack the 
radios) for the purpose of education, in the spirit of furthering the research OR in the 
spirit of punishment.  Some might think that the first reason is positive and the second 
negative.  But, in truth, there probably is not be a clear line of distinction in this instance.  

I realize you did not initially suggest researchers on this board take any action, but you 
did take a strong stance about doing "nothing," (which might be considered an act of 
'inaction' if you think about it) and now you're somewhere on the middle ground ... do 
something, but don't do it on eBay.  All, reasonable positions to take, I might add.  

I understand what you are saying about not wanting to generate negative energy.  I guess 
at this point we are talking about the motivating emotion behind a chosen action as the 
determining factor as to whether or not the same action would be construed as positive or 
negative (or stone throwing, as you call it). 

With ANY of the choices in this matter, there is a corresponding consequence, or 
consequences.  Over the last couple days, a number of options have been thrown out 
there.  The problem is, the situation is complicated, and I don't think there is any one 
correct, or even 'best' solution, because any choice made carries with it some 
responsibility and ownership on the part of the decision maker, including the choice of 
inaction.

In a group a large as this it would likely be impossible to come to a consensus of a "best" 
solution.  And should that monumental task be achieved, and a single best solution 
agreed upon, it would be highly unlikely that there would be a unified motivation behind 
people's opinions as to WHY that solution is best.  

Perhaps the most positive thing that has come from all of this, thus far, is the potential for 
introspection this discussion has brought forth, such as you recognizing your desire to 
"inflict punishment."  What a marvelous gift that insight is. :-)

So, here is what we've come up with so far and the progression of suggestions:

Sell hacked units on eBay at cost or at a small loss

Sell hacked units for profit, but undercut other sellers

Do nothing

Offer hack instructions on eBay for free

Offer hack instructions in more places, but exclude eBay 

Did I miss any?  (probably)

Wow!  Lots for people to think about.  Seems at this point, it is up to each individual 
person to search within, decide for themselves what action (or inaction) will be taken and 
why.  

Margaret
