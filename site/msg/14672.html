Not likely a faulty breaker but it could be. In the winter electrical
devices that heat, such as heat tape and heaters, can do that. It could
also be some other faulty electrical device. It could be a bad wiring
connection or even a bad power meter (I had that happen last winter).

Those touch lamps usually use some sort of CMOS IC or MOSFET device that is
sensitive to capacitance. Touching the base adds capacitance that they sense
and switch as a result. Those devices have a lifetime of maybe 5 to 10 years
from my experience and start switching on their own at random.
