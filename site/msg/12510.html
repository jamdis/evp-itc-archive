Correct, and a lot of that appropriation happened during the several
different inquisitions (1100's through 1600's) where the Catholic
Church first sought to secure its dominance over other sects of
Christianity and then moved on to others over time.  That's where you
have things like the King James Bible appear out of (during the later
part of that period), which pretty much replaced everything with
"devil" and "demon" (which one depends on which version of KJ you read).

And to clarify what I was stating (which is not how Don was taking it)
- in its Jewish origins it has nothing to do with temptation and
making us stronger.  I was not stating anything along those lines. 
Its purely neutral, and an agent of simple choice - i.e. it presents
both good and bad choices equally, i.e. if you have something bad
it'll present something good and vice versa.  The idea kabbalistically
is that we were put here to develop and exercise free will.  You can't
have free will if you don't have choice, hence ha-satan.  I.E, you
can't choose between Coke and Pepsi if there's just Coke or there's
just Pepsi.  The other has to be created and provide to provide
"choice", and therefore enable you to express "free will".  Nothing
negative about that.  The context of negativity is one that was laid
on during appropriation.
