I think Misty makes some good points, and I agree that the issue is similar to how one exposes a child to a belief system, such as a religion.  I agree, too, that it will depend on the individual situation, as some will do this in a healthy way, and some may not: for example, we could all probably name some religious cults that have been more harmful than good.  In the ITC world, I suppose a bad situation would be one where a poor ITC researcher (one who thinks they are getting results, but isn't) insists to the child that they can hear spirits. And I suppose a good situation would be as Misty suggested: where an open mind is presented to them. 

And as Frank noted, this likely also depends on the child. And Frank also mentioned something I thought was interesting: mental health.  What is normal for mental health is often defined as what the majority believes.  Since ITC is still not as big a belief system as other religions or belief systems, it could be seen as abnormal by the uninitiated.  And if Social Services were to get involved (depending on how open minded the worker was) it could result in a messy situation for someone who was insisting they could hear voices talking to them over the radio or other devices. 

Or do you think this is an overreation on my part to what Frank meant there?

I think the answers will vary from individual to individual based on thier beliefs. As for me personally I grew up surrounded by unexplained events and my parents tried to ignore the things that they couldnt see because if you ignore it then it doesnt exist right? But there comes a time when the child grows up and forms thier own ideas and beliefs. Personally speaking once I reached adulthood there were many other people who experienced the same things I did in that house so I began to see that there was more to it than just a childs imagination.

Most religions are based upon the idea that there is a higher power, that we all have spirits, and that there is something beyond this life. To say that someone is wrong for belief in ghosts I would have to say that billions of others are wrong for having belief at all. I dont think bad of a parent who takes their kids off to church every sunday nor do I think badly about a parent who spends some time doing ITC. Telling a child that there is more than what the eye can see, more than what the ears can hear, is giving them the basics for growing up with a mind thats open to possibility. What they do with that is up to them individually.

I worry more about the kids who grow up with parents who are alchoholics, drug addicts, liars, thieves, abusers, etcetera.. than anyone.

As for if the effect is positive or negative, I think that has to do with the intent or the approach taken by each individual whether it be ITC, religion, or life in general.
