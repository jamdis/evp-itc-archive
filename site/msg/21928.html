That's very true, Rick. It is much quieter at night. Birds aren't chirping incessantly and car traffic is much quieter. Also, I think it's easier to see the spirits at night because bright daylight washes some of them out, especially if their forms are pale and not black. Also, you can't really use IR cameras or full spectrum cameras as effectively in bright sunlight. They are much better suited to dimly lit areas. HOWEVER, you CAN use an infrared lens filter over your digital camera lens to capture spirits in broad daylight, and I have seen some very good pictures of full apparitions taken in daylight with no special filters.

There are benefits to doing it both ways, I think. Certainly, EVP is the easiest to do anytime, day or night.
