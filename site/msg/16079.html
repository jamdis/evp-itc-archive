EVP itself defies categorization. Some folks think it is a modulated em field in proximity to the microphone. There is evidence to support this, as evp has been recorded in faraday cages and vacuum jars. It would then be logical to assume that evp is independent of vibratory sound waves. Then that is blown to hell when you consider that Waldemar Bogoras is credited with the first reported evp capture while recording rituals of the Tchoutki tribe in Siberia in 1901. The was with a mechanical gramaphone-like device, no microphone, just a horn to amplify sound, mechanical "write" needle, and wax cylinder. Electric gramaphones didn't come about till almost the '20s. In that case it would appear that evp exists as soundwaves and not em fields.

Which one is right? Both probably, and that isn't even taking into account the mechanism involved in box-related evp.

Intent and desire to communicate. The rest works itself out.
