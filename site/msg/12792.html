Oh yeah, the debate in the Catholic Church philosophical circles that has 
been going on for ages - how many angels can fit on the head of a pin?

Bob Weir even made reference to it in his song "Weather Report Suite."
(Or maybe John Barlow wrote the lyrics.)
:-)

But we are definitely dealing with the non-physical here, so any ideas 
that try to correlate a spirit to something physical can be thrown out the 
window. While it is true that they appear to be coexisting in the physical 
universe, there's no reason to assume they are limited by it.

I have dozens of EVP I've gotten in the former Manteno State Mental 
Hospital in Illinois.  And you know what? Whatever made those EVP's still 
seems to be crazy. And these were apparently made by spirits who no longer 
have physical bodies.  So much for the idea of "chemical imbalances in the 
brain" being the root of insanity! A ghost doesn't have a brain.

I think Hamlet summed it up the best:

"There are more things in heaven and earth, Horatio, than are dreamt of in 
your philosophy."

I constantly remind myself of that.
