Hi Margaret, Misty & all,
 The manual radio sweep can produce some very clear messages. A few tips I have found over the years. My best results were using a radio or stereo that had very good quality of sound reproduction, typically with a large speaker (over 6') or even larger external speakers. The ambiance of the room also helps, try to record in a room with some space. Distance the recorder from the sound source as far as practical. Don't overpower the recorder with loud sound, experiment with different sound levels. Keep everyone else in the house quiet during your sessions, easier said then done :). 
 
 Currently I have been experimenting with light and sweeping heterodyne sound. The method is much like a manual radio sweep except there is no radio signals. The sweep 'noises' are some how transformed to words we can hear. This currenty requires a large tabletop full of test equipment to work. If I can get the voices clearer I will attempt to downsize the method to something the size of a table radio.
