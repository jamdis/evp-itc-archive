Hi Zack.  I know of researchers who've successfully recorded EVP in a shielded 
environment.  It was not done with the assistance of a radio.  Just a simple digital recorder 
in silence.  Well, of course, the recorder makes it own noise.  

I'm *super* non-technical, so I haven't a clue about how any of our EVP gizmos work 
internally.  I just know that I get results from various methods based my own 
experimentation.  ;-)  I did have a friend modify one of my digital recorders.  He replaced 
the microphone with a phototransistor and I have gotten many EVP using this recorder in a 
darkened room with a battery operated plasma globe as the light source.  I first tried one 
that plugs into the wall but every recording was overwhelmed by the 60hz buzz (I think 
that's what it's called?)

Because the modified unit doesn't record what I'm saying, I can't show relevance, but I 
know based on my intention of the session that what I received was appropriate.  My 
favorite being when I asked if what I was visualizing with my own energy field was of any 
help, and I got the reply, "You're developing strength" in an absolutely clear and 
understandable voice.  A lot of what comes through via this method is kinda buried in the 
noise and hard to make out, but this was really easy to make out.  

Tom Butler uses the terms Opportunistic EVP and Transform EVP to describe the 
differences in the theories about various EVP formation.  Transform, among other things, 
being the type that can be received in a completely shielded environment.  Opportunistic 
mostly relating to something like radio sweep or EVPMaker where fragments of speech 
have been selected in order to communicate.  

Have you checked out the web page relating to this?  Here's the link.  It's really long so 
you'll have to copy and paste into your browser:

http://etheric-studies.aaevp.com/best_practices_development/index.php?
title=Locating_EVP_formation_and_detecting_false_positives

I'm a huge fan of radio sweep, simply because my own exploration of the technique has 
produced so many compelling examples of relevant communication that I am absolutely 
convinced of it's validity.  Can't explain it.  Can't prove who it is or how it works.  I think it 
would be great if someday we could understand the principles behind, if for nothing else, 
to build a better box, so to speak.  But I'm satisfied (and utterly fascinated!) at this point by 
the results I get and I'm OK with it involving a little mystery.  

Margaret
