Hi John!  

In my (albiet small) experience, the ability to hear something amid the noise can depend upon several factors:  your own mood, your tiredness (or lack thereof), who you are getting on the other side and how in tune/proficient/emotionally connected they are, and maybe most importantly, the time of day and/or the weather.

Being tired or in a bad mood can affect the results.

Who you get talking to you can affect the results.  If I'm talking to this guy "Steve" (who, by the way, I haven't gotten for quite a while), the messages are always loud and clear, even though I didn't know this guy in real life.  When I talked to my dad, however, I think it took him a while to come through loudly; I think he was there earlier, but I just didn't hear him.  Maybe he was distracted, or maybe there was a learning curve, or maybe he and I don't "resonate" on the same frequency.  Who knows?

The time of day seems to make a huge difference for me.  After dark, radio stations seem more numerous, so the airwaves are crowded -- it makes it ten times harder for me to hear the responses because I can't sift through all the noise.  If the weather is overcast, rainy or foggy, it seems to make it harder, too.  Clear sunny days work the best for me.  Maybe this was the difference between your sessions?

Jill
