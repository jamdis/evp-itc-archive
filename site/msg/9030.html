Hi Jeremy.  My ears prefer a higher tone of voice, so I go with 106%.  If I want to slow down 
the response, to make it a little easier to understand when I'm reviewing/editing the 
session, I change the tempo about -18.  Changing the tempo (not the speed) will slow the 
rate of speech without changing the pitch of the voice.  If you alter speed, it also 
raises/lowers the pitch of the voice.

I believe Dave (if I'm recalling correctly) uses a speed of 75%, but sets his recording 
software to +3 octave to restore proper pitch (at 75% without correction the voice would 
be very low).
