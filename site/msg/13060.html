I understand where Rick is coming from, BUT his suggestion is a good 
example of how to conduct the test IF I were testing those I asked to 
help, which I'm not.  This is not a test, just a means of gathering 
audio with the use of material associated with a plane crash, at a 
location other than where the crash happened.

All I asked them to do is provide five, 30 second audio files which 
they record while using the pieces of debris I will be sending them.
I have faith in the researchers on this forum who have volunteered 
(six people so far have been selected).  

Whatever is found on the audio files will be studied, and since I 
will be the one listening to the recordings, and since I already know 
all the details of the incident, I have not set my mind to 
predetermine anything.

Yes, a different type of test could have been done, and I could have 
hidden all information, but that would not have mattered for this 
effort, since the outcome is to only get the audio files, what ever 
may be found on them.

Thanks to those who have volunteered, I cound still use four more 
people too.
