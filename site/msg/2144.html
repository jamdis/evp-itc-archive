I've often wondered abut this.  It seems others have often gotten EVP 
where I didn't, at the same time and place.

I got a LOT of them at the former Manteno State Mental Hospital (Illinois) 
last year though.  I got the same ones on both a pocket DVR and a 
camcorder. Most were Class B EVP because they were barely perceptible. 
Being a radio amateur who has spent hundreds of hours digging weak voices 
out of the noise helps to hear them, though. I did get a few Class A's.

Some of the things I heard were like these.  When I write "questioner" I 
mean someone in the group who asked the question.  When I write "EVP" I 
mean the response, which was not from anyone in the group.

Questioner: "Are there other spirits here with you?" / EVP: "You bet" 
(male voice, slightly speeded up)

Questioner: "Are you happy?" / EVP: "Course I'm haaaapeeee" (long 
drawn-out male voice, almost slowed down)

Questioner: Do you know what year it is?" / EVP: "1996" (female voice, 
normal speed, very dim. The year was in fact 2006).

Questioner: "Do you like it here?" / EVP: "Leave this building!" (normal 
male voice but quite dim and almost in noise)

Two group members chatting, suddenly a very speeded up word thrown in / 
EVP: "Fantastic". (I slowed it to half-speed with Audacity, then it 
sounded like a normal male voice with a slight accent - Italian?)

Group member commenting on shadow figure seen in adjacent part of building 
through window: "... I don't think it's a person, at least living." / EVP: 
"It's Harold Wing".  Group member "I can still see it moving" / EVP: "It's 
Harold Wing".  Group member makes another comment / EVP "It's Harold." 
(This was a Class A EVP, sounding like a very loud whisper or hoarse male 
voice).

The Manteno State Mental Hospital was closed in 1985.  There's a lot of 
info here: http://www.mantenostatehospital.com

The building we were in was the last remaining building of the original 
hospital still standing that hasn't been renovated or destroyed - the 
Morgan Building.  A researcher found out that there was a patient there in 
1936 named Arnold Wing who died of malaria or typhoid.

Verrry interesting stuff!

Zack
