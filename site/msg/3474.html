You know, though, this same shit has happened to every group of  
paranormal researchers since the field began. I've watched group  
after group come together and explode for thirty years because only  
the Chosen had the right to control the message, would broke no  
criticism of *any* of their own results *ever*, but had plenty of  
definitive pronouncements about everyone else's.

This is why I scoff at the idea of "Harmony," with the capital H. I  
have seen the concept used by everyone from the Warrens in the  
60s-70s to the Harsch-Fischbachs in the early 80s to Moon today  
trying to use this idea of being somehow chosen and sacred in order  
to get "real" messages from spirit. Harmony is a way to control who  
gets to play and IMHO, it's a load of nonsense.

Bruce, I have to tell you that the message you got the other day  
about us being all Kumbaya set my alarms off, perhaps innocently, but  
nonetheless. I've found there is such a fine line between real peace  
between brothers working for the real common good and peace between  
brothers as a mechanism of control. Yes, we need to be at peace to  
work together. But that peace has to be an inclusive peace. It has to  
be a peace within one's self above all else. It needs to be a  
*fearless* interior peace, too. The Harmony is within, folks. It's  
not judging whether others have it or not before we let them play. It  
is pure, unadulterated *internal* knowledge about one's self. Judge  
not, lest ye be judged--remember that one?
