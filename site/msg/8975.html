Hi Laura.  I use Audacity to record box/radio sweep sessions, but I find that using it for 
'traditional' EVP purposes hasn't worked for me.  When I do traditional EVP sessions, I use 
any of my digital recorders (which is where the EVP seem to be formed best, not when 
using the computer) then play the file from my recorder into my computer, so I can better 
review the content of the session as well as edit, save, share, etc.  So yes, you still need a 
patch cable.  :-)

I think the confusion may have been when you told them at Radio Shack that you wanted 
to connect your mic of the recorder to the mic of your computer??  What you want to do is 
connect the EAR/HEADPHONE to the computer.  So, instead of listening through the 
speaker of the recorder, or plugging in headphones and listening with your ears, you are 
going to play the file and let your computer hear it through the cable.  Hope that makes 
sense.

Just press the record button on Audacity, then press the play button on your recorder, and 
it will start recording in the computer.  Then save your session.  After that you can edit, 
isolate areas, export WAV files, etc.  If you want to be able to export MP3 format, you have 
to download/install the LAME add-on for Audacity.  

Margaret
