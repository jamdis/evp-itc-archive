>  TRF=tuned radio frequency. The kind of radio that was in use when radio
> was first invented. There is a modern chip version, it has three pins and is
> very simple to set up. TRF has the advantage of less noise and static, but
> in a box the stations tend to over lap, but it still can give good results.
> It's the same radio in earlier version of the Mini box, and Chapple also
> used it in one of his projects. I have found it's best to wind the coil and
> tweak it to the radio by removing tunrs until it covers the AM band. I start
> with 70 turns of wire, the remove five at a time--or you could just wind
> sixty turns and leave it at that, but being anal I tend to over do it.
