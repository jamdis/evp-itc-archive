Hi all, OK, I'll try to write down the steps necessary to synthesize text by combining allophones. In this example I will show how to synthesize the word "hello". 1. Open SJ-Allophone.wav in Audition. 2. Show the Cue List, if not already opened, by selecting "View > Show Cue List" from the menu. 3. Look up the first allophone of "hello", an "h" with transition to "e". Choose the allophone "HE" as the first one. Double-click it in the Cue List - it will get selected in the waveform window. To listen to it, click the Play button. To copy the selected allophone to a new file, press , and then . The allophone will open in a new window. Press to set the cursor at the end of the waveform. Press to return to the SJ-Allophones window. 4. Look up the next allophone for the word "hello", which is the "e" sound. The (probably) correct allophone is labeled "EH", so double-click it, listen to it by clicking Play, and if it's the correct one, press to copy it to the clipboard, then press to switch to the new created file, press to insert the allophone from the clipboard at the and of the new file (cursor position). Press to set the cursor at the end of the waveform, then again to return to the SJ-Allophones window. 5. Repeat Step 4 for the remaining two allophones of "hello", which are labeled "LO" and "OWWW". So the word "hello" is composed of the allophones: HE + EH + LO + OWWW 6. Save the new file. In my example, I saved it as "SJ-Allophones - hello.wav" and can be downloaded/listened to here: http://tech.groups.yahoo.com/group/EVP-ITC/files/Stefan/Speech%20Recognition%20Training/SJ-Allophones%20-%20hello.wav Stefan -- > Good stuff aaron.
