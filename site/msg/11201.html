I would suggest as much a controlled experiment as possiable. Because our recorders are so sensitive it should be in a place where only the 'readers' would be heard. I would keep the 'scripts' under two minutes. Try to place some distance from the readers and the microphone (at least 20 feet). A room with a slight echo should work best. Keep everyone in a 'testing' frame of mind. As an example the scripts could be three different classic poems everyone would be familier with. As you know, frame of mind and intent will be very important to get any cooperation.

Good Luck,

I'll add my two cents here for what it's worth. The term I have heard in many of my own sessions is 'Heterodyne'. They somehow combine/mix multiple sounds, frequencies and or energy to produce the voices. A good test of this is to have three people reading from different scripts (for control) at the same time, ask your questions while all three are speaking and listen for a loud response in the combined voices in the playback. http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heterodyne
