RS 20125 Hack:

Bruce,

Yes, I just finished hacking one, too.  It definitely is harder.  The 
biggest thing I can recommend is to not remove the bottom board.  If 
you can tear only the front section of the radio and keep the bottom 
intact, you will fare better.  Simply undo the top little board that 
has the LCD window (4 clips), flip it back, and punch out the 7th 
wire from the right.  A Bruce mentioned, you will need to be careful 
with the two little buttons up top for the light and SW button (that 
you don't need). You may be able to hold them in place with a 
toothpick for each one when you pop the board up.  If you lose them 
in the radio, don't worry about it.  Let it go and re-assemble.  The 
radio will work just fine for you.

As Steve mentioned earlier, the shortwave sweeps slow, unless you 
hold it in.  The AM and FM sweep like the RS12-470 (older version).  
Clean sweep - nice unit.  If I get some time, I took some pictures of 
what it looks like opened up.  I can post them on here for you to 
see.  Are we gonna' call this "Steve's Box," or the RS Hack 20125?
