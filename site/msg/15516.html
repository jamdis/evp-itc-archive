This RVG business is really perplexing, and I hope you can find a way to stabilize it, because random modes sound the best to me (maybe it is a nostalgic bias, lol).

Here are some sources I found about random number generation (which I assume could be adapted for RVG use) based on diode noise.



http://www.accu-noise.com/ (suppliers)

http://www.ciphersbyritter.com/NOISE/NOISRC.HTM (really cool rng schematics based off of zener noise)

http://noisecom.com/products_noise_diodes.asp (noise diodes and such)

http://www.psylab.com/html/default_tg2toneg.htm (wng/tone generator)

http://randomnumber.org/links.htm (resource site with cool links)

http://world.std.com/~reinhold/waynesrngcomp.gif (off the shelf noise generator schematic, not sure if it or any part of it would be applicable)

http://www.maxim-ic.com/appnotes.cfm/appnote_number/3469 (another WNG schematic, but it looks like voltages may be a bit high for box use)
