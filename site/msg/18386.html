There are things we can measure and things we can't measure, whether or not
we can perceive them without instruments.  I can measure electromagnetic
signals in the range of 15 kHz to beyond untraviolet. In many cases I can
tell the frequency/wavelength, the field strength at the antenna/receiver,
the source or at least the direction of source, and the type of any
modulation present. But I can't see, hear or otherwise sense these with my
body alone.

With equipment costing millions or billions of dollars, we can detect
electrons, protons, neutrons, bosons, hadrons, leptons etc.  These things
all behave in certain ways and respond the same way each time.

Spirits are something we cannot definitely measure at this time. For one
thing, they have intellect and a power of choice, something a radio signal
does not have. They are unpredictable. And although people carry around EMF
meters, cameras, digital audio recorders, etc., there is no guarantee that a
spirit will leave some response on any of these things and there is no
guarantee that the responses gotten are from spirits. Look at all the people
who think all the dust scatterings and insects they get in photos and videos
are "orbs."

And there are no instruments yet that can measure a person's level of
bravery, honesty, integrity, etc.

There's an awful lot we know but an awful lot we don't know. But, as Frank
mentioned, I'm not doing any of this for anyone else. I'm not trying to
prove anything. I'm just curious for myself.

Zack
