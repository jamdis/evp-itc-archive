>  The speaker in on one "end" of the echo chamber tube, and the mic is one
> the other, In this case I glued thin plastic sheet from something I bought
> in a blister pack over the ends of the tubes, then cut a hole the diameter
> of the tube for the speaker, and for the mic I melted a hole with a
> soldering iron, then glued mic and speaker in place.. I have used all
> kinds of stuff for the chamber, 12 inch X 4 foot long MDF board chamber,
> aluminum foil in a 1/4 inch wood frame, metal channels formed by gluing in
> metal strips. In box #1, I used a triangle shaped cany tin, and hte main
> board for the box was mounted on top.
> 
>  The mic feed the fourth op amp channel of the sweep generator, where it's
> amplified and forms the line out signal, and then drives the monitor/panel
> speaker. The electret mic has to be biased to work, if you buy one at RS I
> think the paskage shows the details. it gets a little complicated.
