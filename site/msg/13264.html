Hello Ron, and thanks for the interesting reply. I have posted a few times before in this forum which I think have answered your question about my experiences.  They would be under Rick or Rj.  To summarize, I have not posted any clips, and I do not have any results that I would make any claims about at all.  My interests here are more in the areas of process and methodologies.

I have gathered, as you noted, that may of the members here aren't really doing anything scientific, but are more in the hobbyist or enthusiast mode.  That's cool.  I know of people who like to go fishing, just to enjoy the company, or the alone-time, and don;t want to catch anything or produce anything either, but just enjoy the time.  I'm certainly not trying to tell people what to do with their time!

But just in cast there is someone reading, who might like to use some methodology to help them test and refine their ideas, or who is not just one of the hobbyists to which you allude, but trying to be a researcher who can show objective results, or hopes to make progress with their innovation, I present these ideas.  They are, actually, not what one would call a full blown scientific methodology (since in this case, that would require first proving that there are spirits out there before trying to claim that they are what are shaping the noises on your device).  But they are a tool to help direct anyone who is trying different techniques, and looking for an objective way to determine which techniques seem to be the most promising.  I don't think this is looking for proof of anything, or trying to debunk anything.  It's just trying to assess experimental results, which in this field I understood can be tricky.  As Misty noted, different people can hear different things than she does, and its a nice surprise when they do hear the same thing.  Maybe it would be an even nicer surprise if, under the conditions I outlined, a lot of people heard the same things!

Again, I am not trying to advocate debunking your creativity or ideas. But for anyone who is looking for some new tools, this time in the realm of process and methodology, to help them with their explorations, I'd be happy to offer more ideas there.  Were there any points in my process Ron that you took exception to, don't find value in, or think can be improved?,

I know what you mean Misty - it does seem that interpretations are often varied.  I am thinking that the best way to make progress is to get an audio sample that you think is most representative of a given approach's effectiveness, and send that privately to several people.  Name the file something innocuous (like File1) and don';t tell the people you send it to what you think you hear.  Also, ask the people not to discuss the file with anyone else, and have them send what they think they hear directly back to you,rather than posting the results in a forum or group.

I think that kind of test is helpful, since it seems to me that once someone tells me what the audio clip sounds like, then it's hard for me to hear anything else in it.  But,that could be influencing my interpretation (like someone pointing to a cloud, telling me its a horse, and then I have trouble NOT seeing a horse) ,so it's better to listen before it is suggested to me what i should be hearing.

This way, I think the most progress can be made, because if people just keep confirming what we think we hear, and it turns out it's just noise that we are forcing an interpretation into, we won't be making progress (though it might make us feel good to believe that we're being successful).  Getting negative feedback to one's approach is not as fun, and might feel like some non-believer is trying to debunk us.  But, really, that can be the best feedback for progress, since it helps us to change tactics when our current approach is not effective, rather than continuing down an ineffective path, getting accolades from friends, but missing out on other paths that might show more promise,.
