A couple of weeks ago, I had an amazing opportunity to spend some time
with Mark Macy. He's an ITC researcher who has published several works
on the subject. He, along with my husband, Rosemary Ellen Guiley, and
I spent some time with the MiniBox and I have to tell you it was a
pretty incredible experience. Naturally, when I got home, I purchased
two of Macy's books on ITC. The first was 'Miracles in the Storm', and
not far into it, I came across a couple of quotes I thought would be
fitting to share.

'...psychic channeling involves a blending of minds of the spirit
communicator and the human channel, as a result the message received
has been "filtered' by the mind of the channel. Very rarely does a
message come through a human channel in a pure and unfiltered form.
With ITC, on the other hand, messages from spirit can come through the
equipment virtually unfiltered by the minds of the experimenters. So,
ITC provides a more reliable means of communication with the worlds of
spirit...'

If it is indeed possible to communicate with other realms through our
electronics, in my opinion, there would not be a need for exclusivity
in who could actually receive the communication. We all stand to
benefit greatly (and I am not speaking monetarily) if we can 'bridge'
the gaps between our reality and whatever lies beyond it.

I have always been taught that there is 'opposition in all things'.
For every positive, there is a negative and vice versa. We, as a
collective of like-minded researchers could make some pretty amazing
discoveries and share them for the greater good. We'll have to take
the negatives in stride.

Sorry if it seems I am waxing philosophical... I just believe there is
a huge potential for growth here. Bravo to anyone is seeking out and
creating new ways of communication without immediately rejecting new
ideas.

Enlightenment is not meant for a select few. As human beings, we are
all entitled. Just my two cents...
