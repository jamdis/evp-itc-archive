Dual supply, linear sweep, treble-bass filter, audio amps. *Tuner is a
separate board*, so this layout is not dependent on what tuner is available.
Me thinks I am vastly underestimated. Cellphone camera--crappy! Dual
supply=it needs  + and - 12 volts to function. Two 12 volts supplies--two
8-AA battery packs or two 12V gel cells, or NiMh packs--or what ever.

 BTW, I have a random sweep board too, the boards are designed to stack  :-)
and no, I can't mass produce these either. But... 3 boards for $85 from
ExpressPCB, solder mask and silk screened, just add components and shake.
There's a few SMT resistors and caps, but that's pretty easy.  PBCs are
easy, the only hold up is tuners. 1/8th watt resistors and standard caps can
be used in the tone section. I know, about impossible to see. Tuners are the
problem. Thios was designed to be used wiht car tuners, but any tuner will
work. There is a three input mixer section that combines the left and right
stereo channels, and an AM channel from a seperate tuner if needed. But
wait, there's more, it has the mic amp for the echo chamber as well. One
would have to supply all components, controls and switches. This is not a
simple project.

 Chris Moon's latest box is done with the PCBs and a RS Arm Band tuner, his
daughter's uses a car tuner with the same PCBs. It's two years old. Not the
first printed boards I have done.

 I usually do perf board and point to point wiring to save money. I should
be doing kits or something.

 FYI, the tone control I found on the web, just so you are aware when some
one tries to bootleg the boards. Ripping off someone's designs may have
consequences-that's why I don't sell these, paying royalties reduces my
labor profit from 5 cents an hour to 2 cents an hour.

 The point is, *if you have tuners*, excess money, and want a spiffy looking
box, you can have PCBs made.

 Sooooo, at the risk of everyone wanting to order these----I can't take
orders for PCBs, I don't have the tuners, and I don't have the $$$$$$ extra
for boards.  The boxes I sold for very reduced prices at Rolling Hills is
what financed these boards. I don't make any money on boxes, I barely get
enough to buy parts, and labor? Forget about it.
