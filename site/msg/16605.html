I tried again the next night and asked Brad WHY he did what he did---His one word reply was "Crazy"  he did not elaborate.  Brad was like that though-- Often very "deadpan" not much for words.
  We had great conversations about the paranormal when he was alive.  He HAD been known for episodes of "instability" though. The night he died---Back in 2002, he woke up ranting and raving to his Girlfriend, jumped in his car and raced down the street.  Unfortunately, a train was crossing it at the time, and he ran his car under it-- without stopping.  I could feel him for years urging me to develop some kind of "electrical" communication with him,(really a long term goal of mine) but I never got around to it. Then I discovered this "Franks Box" stuff a few months back and finally decided to make something like it when I found the hack on the web a few weeks back.  
 I have been working with the components, radio, resonator, noise source, recorder, just layed out on my dining room table and playing with it for a few days, planning to box it up in a transportable unit---Then I saw on TV how someone was using a large PVC pipe to both house the radio and form a resonator.--The idea was great.  by cutting a slot to fit the radio in the side of the pipe the radio fit in prfectly, with the speaker end INSIDE the pipe and it's controls , on its upper half, on the outside of the pipe. I did the same thing with the noise generator, right next to the radio. I turned it on and decided to ask questions.  Bradley finally came to mind and I asked if he was there---Thats when I heard him say his name and I asked what happened to him the night he died.  I heard "Accident" and some other stuff I could not make out.  Unfortunately, I was not running the sound recorder at the time.  The next night I asked the question again and got "Crazy".  (still no recorder)  I have just figgured out how to use a rubber band to secure my small panasonic digital recorder into one end of the tube, but I have not done a session with it in the last couple of days.  My next step is to download the audacity software in my computer so I can process the recordings I get THEN I will be in business.
