Most all of my audio files are in .wav format and are between 10 to 
50kb; many of the mp3 files I am looking at in other folders on here 
far exceed my file sizes (100, 200, 300kb in size).

I propose setting a (specific) kb file size limit, and delete those 
that are over it.  Bigger files can be linked to if uploaded to 
esnips.  Allow both .wav and .mp3 files that meet the requirement.  
Making them all mp3 will not solve the issue.

This way we do not have to convert everything to mp3 format when mp3s 
are not always opening up for everyone easily, either.  I have to do 
a "save target as" to download and save it to my desktop first; wav 
files play automatically.
