I don't wish to belabor this point too much further since it is not the focus of this forum. However this is so central to understanding phenomenology and PK does appear to be a potential root cause of at least some EVP and ITC work, so one more comment is in order.

As part of my research I reanalyzed many previously reported cases. I found a remarkable number where PK (including remote PK) was a quite plausible explanation. 

Consider also that in my work this is highly replicable phenomena. Replicable phenomena is defined as having a larger effect size than subtle less replicable phenomena. Many forms of mediumship and related psi are correctly defined as small effect size phenomena and hence far less replicable. This leads to the requirement to conduct extensive testing simply to establish correct statistical outcomes.

However as replicability improves so too does effect size (boiling water being an excellent example of a large effect size phenomenon that is of course highy replicable). As effect size and replicability increase the 'rarity' of phenomena is no longer supported. Therefore I would contend, based on the above that indeed remote PK is in fact far more common than is commonly believed  and in fact this nicely explains not a small percentage but in fact a large percentage of many anecdotally reported phenomena.
