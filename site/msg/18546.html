>  I make the triangle sweep adjustable so I can cover the entire AM or FM
> band. Some tuners have different sweep requirements for each band, so
> sometimes have to add a pot to adjust the max level for one of the bands.
> AM=530 to 1710 Khz and FM 88.5 to 108mhz, but you don't have to cover the
> entire band, all that is required is enough to provide the raw audio bits
> the entities use to make their voices.
> 
>  A Danish researcher back in the 80s was just "wobbling" between a couple
> stations to make contact. His name was Stein and there's more info on the
> German VTF site. Stein also has his sweep circuit there.
> 
>  The best sounding systems seem to be the way Steve Hultay makes his, by
> adding sweep to a complete radio. see keyportparanormal.com. I take the
> tuners out of the radio, and supply my own amplifier and sweep. At present
> I'm making a box using a Timex clock radio tuner, the triangle sweep, and
> computer sound amplifier and speaker system for the bass response. I just
> started this project yesterday. I use Timex model T309T AM/FM/nature sounds
> clock radios when I can find them in thrift stores cheap. I found a Timex
> model T313T this morning, and hoping it has the seperate tuner board inside,
> as the 300 series has so far.
> 
>  I used to use car stereo tuner modules, but those tend to be too noisy. Box
> 13, that Hultay just got back is based on a car tuner. He did a YouTube
> video of it.
> 
>  I'm still pretty much an amateur at this, there are so many variables.
> 
>  Frank-S
