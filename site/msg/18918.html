Adriana,

The vulgar ones come through from time to time for everyone.   I have found
that location does not matter, it just seems to be that if someone wants to
swear, they will.  I used to get vulgarity quite frequently, but then I
started addressing it directly.  If I hear a swear word, or if I am working
with a group and the Others start picking on someone, I tell them to knock
it off and that being vulgar is not conducive to communication.  If they
continue, I threaten to turn off the box and this seems to quiet them down.
 Once I started doing this, the vulgarity has almost ceased in my
communications.

But, on the other hand, vulgarity is exactly the kind of proof that we have
that there is something to ghost box communication.  Title 18 of the US Code
prohibits the utterance of "any obscene, indecent or profane language by
means of radio communication."  So when these vulgarities appear, you can
pretty much rule out any radio broadcast over the standard AM/FM bands.
 This, in my mind and many other GB researchers, constitutes communication.


Your best bet is to try to coax them into limiting their use of profanity
and hopefully it will work for you.  Be sure to document the swears though!
 As unfortunate as it is, these are some of the most credible pieces of
evidence.

Tim
