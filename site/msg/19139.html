Our group tried the various Raudive Diode's for over five years and it
yielded precisely nothing.

The Raudive Diode was, at the time of the book Breakthrough, a new technique
that was being developed by an american physicist named Stanley that he was
working with.  The forward of the book was written by a women who worked
with Raudive shortly after the book was published.  She recounts how
Konstantin plugged the diode in between the radio and the recorder and
was embarrassed when it did not work.  Eventually it did work, but the
antennae length had to be constantly adjusted.  This basically creates a
very primitive radio crystal set.  The diode really came to light thanks to
George Meek who claims that he modified it for the Spiricom.

So does it work?

Not for us, and apparently, it worked very rarely for Raudive.

Tim
