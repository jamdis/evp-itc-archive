Hi Stefan.  I also think keeping a raw version on file is really important.  I'm somewhere in 
the middle about editing.  I'm OK with it (obviously, I do it myself) but feel that the fact 
editing has taken place should be disclosed when sharing examples.  Too often people 
present edited clips with no mention whatsoever that changes have been made, which can 
lead to false impressions being given to listeners.  

I think of removing 'garbage' in sweep and related techniques as being similar to removing 
all the silence in traditional EVP work.  Nobody wants to listen to 45 seconds (or however 
long) of hiss if that's how long it took for the response to come.  And to the untrained ear. 
raw sweep examples can be very difficult for the average person to understand without 
editing that reflects the way we are hearing it.

Granted, with sweep, we are actually removing material we have deemed as irrelevant, not 
just silence. Hopefully everyone has the good sense to not rearrange content, or take a 
response that came after several questions and apply it to the first.  In general, I feel an 
appropriate response must be received prior to the next question or comment being 
made. 

I save all my raw files in WAV format, and my edited versions in MP3.  As well, any raw 
versions that I post will be MP3 in order to use as little server space as possible.  :-)

Again, I am with you, Stefan, in that we should always be able to present our results to a 
critical public.  For me, that means keeping raw files for comparison, and disclosing when 
I have edited an example in order to hopefully avoid any confusion.  And since I've been up 
front about it, I can't be accused of deceptive practices.

Glad you shared your views!

Margaret

> just a general comment on editing EVP: For scientific reasons, I would
> suggest not to edit an EVP message partially, but only as a whole, in
> order to rule out any possible suspicion of manipulation. You can for
> instance increase the volume or dynamics of an entire clip (including
> the experimenter's voice), filter or denoise it to make it clearer, but
> not cut out individual pieces (supposed "garbage") to make an EVP match
> with what it "should be". At least, the unedited version should always
> be provided along with the edited version.
> 
> But that's only my personal opinion which I thought I had to express, as
> we should always be able to present our results to a critical public.
