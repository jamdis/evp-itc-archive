Titanium is one of the worst metal conductors, around 1.8 E06 S/m. Stainless
steel is in that same range.

I wouldn't bother using anything but copper for most electronics
applications. I will silver plate RF circuits I build for VHF-microwave uses
but that's far beyond what's needed for anything like a ghost box. Plain old
solid or stranded copper wire and copper circuit board material would do
fine, I think. Plus it's a lot cheaper. No reason to get expensive if you
don't need to.

Zack
