I can produce very high-quality boards.  I recently made 8 boards for a 
ham radio millimeter-wave project - 47 and 78 GHz mixers. They were etched 
on 5-mil thick Duroid 5870. That stuff is like paper. The tolerances at 
those frequencies is incredibly small. Hopefully they work. They look like 
they should.

But run-of-the-mill boards I have no problem with.  I can make boards on 
1/16 inch thick G-10 fiberglass-epoxy board for a couple dollars each. As 
I mentioned, the only drawback is with double-sided boards. I have to use 
feedthrough wires instead of plated-through holes.

If you (Frank) have a circuit pattern some time that you're willing to let 
me try, I can make a few boards and we can see how they work.

Zack
