Hi Kristin.  I think our perceptions and expectations really have tremendous influence on 
our experiences.  "The Experimenter Effect" so to speak.  I would venture to guess, that 
prior to taking on any kind of box, someone had already planted the notion in your mind 
that the box/radio is a portal of sorts and you conducted your session accordingly.  I find 
that those in spirit are more than willing to go along with whatever frame of reference we 
are using.  I often suspect, that in some ways, we are making up the guidelines for their 
communication with us, and they comply.  

If your belief is that you have opened a portal, then ask questions based on that belief (ie:  
are you back on your side? or things along that line) then they will answer accordingly.  
They typically don't respond with questions like "what are you talking about?"  ;0)  

I purposefully limited my exposure to the multitudes of opinions floating around 
cyberspace until I had worked for many months with various sweeps, and for the most 
part had formed my own opinions based solely on my personal experiences.  Only then 
did I venture out to see what others might be saying. 

I will say, as common courtesy would have it, I do officially close my sessions by saying 
things like, "OK, I'm going to listen now.  Thanks for talking.  If you have anything left to 
say, please do it now, you have XX seconds."  (10, 20, 30, whatever, timewise)  Then I turn 
off the recorder, the box, the radio, or whatever I'm using.  I have done this since day one 
just because I've always thought of EVP communication as being similar to talking with 
someone in the room, or on the phone.  As with any conversation, it's deserving of a 
proper closure of some sort.

One thing I did find that is absolutely spot on with many other's experiences is, that there 
appears to be technical people helping us.  But I had long ago come to this conclusion, 
way before any sweep experimentation came into the picture for me,  because in my 
traditional EVP work I was getting technical messages.  

I didn't hear the rest of your clip as clearly as the end, but I *totally* know what you mean 
about how something can be so clear to you but not to others.  I think this is just part of 
the mystery of EVP, sweep, etc.  Perhaps some of it is a telepathic connection of sort to our 
communicators, and also likely to contribute to the differences is that we are all using 
countless combinations of computer hardware/software, speakers, etc.  

I once conducted an experiment where I burned 10 or so EVP's to CD.  I then used that 
same CD in various systems.  I was absolutely stunned at the difference it sometimes 
made.  Even on the same system, using different software applications can cause 
examples to be heard slightly differently. But in some cases, the difference was dramatic 
from one system combination to the next.  

I suspect one reason for this is, when we are conducting our sessions, the communication 
is being given in a way that it is optimized for that particular hardware/software setup.  Of 
course, the higher the clarity of original message content to begin with, the less influence 
system variations will have on other's abilities to hear an example as we are.  Likewise, I 
believe it is possible that the communication could even be tailored to our particularly 
hearing.  We all have different ranges of hearing and sounds that are better, or less, suited 
to those ranges.  

OK, I'm way off on a rambling spell here.  It's like my fingers have a life of their own when 
I'm at the keyboard sometimes!!  

Margaret
