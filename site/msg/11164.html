You'll probably get a lot of different responses as to favorites but I've 
been very happy with the Olympus VN-960PC I got a few years ago. It comes 
with a USB cable that plugs into your computer.

It will record 6 hours in hi-quality mode.

Radio Shack sells newer versions like the VN-4100PC which probably have 
more recording time than you will ever need on a single investigation.

Some other Olympus models actually allow you to use the flash memory built 
in for storing and transferring data via the USB port.  The VN-XXXPC 
series only allow you to download the recordings you've made to your PC. 
I might consider getting one of the fancier ones some day. Check out the 
Radio Shack website for further details. But I think they all use built-in 
flash and don't use separate CF or SD cards.

> Al, this is the other Al, heh. I like the Olympus and have three of them and one Sony. IMHO the Olympus beats Sony hands down. Model WS-300M is my favorite. I believe it runs 24-hours -- I use it for overnight evps in houses and have captured very clear sounds. It is stereo and snaps apart to convert into a memory stick complete with usb. You can store data/text files and music, and the tiny speaker is excellent. This thing is so light a good Cuban cigar weighs more. I believe these go for $150 or so, but I got this one new on ebay for $65. Another good one I use all the time is Olympus VN-960PC, which may not be in mfg. any more. It records about 16 hours and is slightly larger, but has a darn good mike and I like the clip for the shirt pocket. Also it has a lighted display (as does the ws-300m) thhat's larger than the other one, consequently with bigger figures for easier reading. You didn't ask for this, but the one I'd avoid is the Sony icd-st25. The sound is nowhere near true and though it has most of the features of the Olympus models the sound quality is first in my book. I think all of these are available at wally world, costco, and circuit city, though you can usually save a lot at ebay if you're patient.
