Who to go to?  The scientific method!
This was a good example of how a couple people, with differing opinion= s but open minds, can follow an objective methodology to root out the right= answer.

1) Observation.  We heard (indirectly) that someone ha= s seen wave files experience lossy compression when sent through email clie= nts.

2) Hypothesis.  The theory is that the email client, and/o= r email servers, perform lossy compression on their attachments for wave fi= les.

3) Predictions.  Based on this hypothesis, this should be = observable by attaching and sending large waves files through some email se= rvers, and comparing both the originals, and the final files.  If orig= inal files and the final files should experience differences in contents.
4) Experiments.  We sent different files, of different sizes, t= hrough different clients and servers. 

5) Analyze results.&nbs= p; We compared the originals at the byte level, and found no loss.

6= ) Interpret.  No compression was observed in our tests, and so no comp= ression is being done with the servers and clients and environments in whic= h we tested.  This does not make any claims about other tests or envir= onments, but provides good testing and predictions for what would be expect= ed based on our tests.

Without this kind of method, we could have ju= st gone back and forth giving subjective or emotional arguments, with no re= al evidence.  But following this kind of method, we get to what we rea= lly both wanted: reliable interpretation of phenomenon.

And this doe= sn't mean we need to close the books forever.  More prediction, more e= xperiments, more results can be tried by more people, published, and reprod= uced.  The body of knowledge then grows wider, deeper, and more relia= ble.

This is why I push the scientific method even with EVP studies,= even while others continue subjective or emotional arguments: to improve t= he study, and to allow progress to be made by as many people as possible.
