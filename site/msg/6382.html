Nothing has been cut at all, Steve. The first take is exactly as it  
came over the radio--no amplification, no nothing. The repeat is  
exactly the same except slowed. Nothing is missing from in between  
the words.

I've been questioning how my minibox is working myself. It seems to  
be behaving differently from other people's. Some of this may be that  
I live in the Baltimore-Washington broadcasting area and we have  
literally a hundred radio stations. Trying to get a sweep where it  
isn't almost constant fragments is impossible. Mike, Lisa, et al may  
be sending things through to me differently than others. I don't  
know. But when I slow what I get by about 50 percent to listen on  
playback, this is what starts coming up--not swearing and all, mind  
you, but a lot of words coming with each pulse of broadcast that make  
sense when put together--too much sense to be random. At least that  
is how I am seeing it.

The other clip I posted, "We're in heaven," I only found because I  
was trying to identify a nearby word and when I slowed it down to a  
literal crawl, "We're in heaven" is what I found--waay too clear to  
be random, again.

Tomorrow I will run a sample of the kind of raw sound I get when I  
turn my box on.

Believe me, I'm confused about the speed, too. It isn't what I was  
expected to happen with the mini. But it sure is wild stuff.

I don't think the voices sound evil. I think they are just people.  
They don't do anything to disrupt my life, which is very pleasant and  
happy. Ever since I've lived here, however, I've been aware that  
there is at least one male entity in the cemetery across the street.  
I call him the Grumpy Old Man. I can't get him to leave, but I have  
made a kind if deal with him to let each other go unmolested ;) This  
may be him. I just don't know. I know I am going to go looking for  
Andrews or Stus over there tomorrow, however.

Ann
