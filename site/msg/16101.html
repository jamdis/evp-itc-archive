I tend to agree regarding the definitions of beliefs. There seems to be a continuum: at each end are people with closed minds who operate from a dogma of philosophy and beliefs. In each case their positions are relatively unshakable. One end is the staunch believer and the other end is what I and others have come to call the 'pseudoskeptic'. It is this latter type of person who often labels themselves a 'skeptic' and who tries to argue from what they term positions of 'logic', say they are 'critical thinkers' and demand 'evidence' placing their faith in their version of 'science'. All of these terms are of their own definition and are maleable depending on the situation. In the center of this spectrum are what I think of as the true and only 'open-minded critical thinkers'. I suppose one could label these individuals as 'skeptics' but this kind of clouds the issue. I am sad to say that I really believe the size of this third group is well under 1/3 of everyone. Just my opinion of course.
