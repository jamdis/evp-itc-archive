Frank, I'm not misleading anyone, the optical microphone using a flame in and of itself works very well in the capture of Spirit voices. I don't know exactly why, after almost two years I am still studying it although I can speculate it could because there is a lot going on during it's use. Change of state from liquid to vapor to heat and light, IR radiation, thermal noise and some processes I am missing or unaware of. The radio sweep is used as a source of sound samples, the microphone also works well using music or plain speech. The limited high frequency response also seems to act as a built-in bandpass filter. I'm not using anything complicated or out of the reach of a Wal-Mart or K-Mart shopper. I'm using an off the shelf oil lamp with a 3/4 inch wide flat wick, a phototransistor from Radio Shack and an FM radio.  There is more to the flame microphone then modulated light, I can modulate the DC feeding a flashlight or and LED and not get the same results. Here is an exchange using the optical microphone and FM radio sweep, my voice is reduced by 10db.

Response : "Fourty Seven"

Dave: 'Please state your name, thank you'

Response : "Ingrid"
