Those are the IF, or intermediate frequency transformers, they are set 
at the factory to pass a 455khz signal. Radio staions are converted 
from whatever frequency they are on to the 455khz IF freq. for better 
amplification, ans selectivity. When you adjust those you're screwig 
with the IF pass band, which usually is not a good thing. You'll 
probably notice other parts of the band no longer receive, or stations 
over lap. 
So Now you need a 455khz oscillator, tune a staiton, and adjust the 
transformer for strongest beat signal. 

 The red core is the local oscillator, the white and black the IF 
transformers. So you'll also need a freq counter to get the thing back 
in tune. 

 Tehre is a sot onthe board where a wire can be added that helps the AM 
reception a little, but I don't think I'd recommend screwing with it.

 I think get a book on basic radio fundamentals.
