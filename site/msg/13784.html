I have had great luck with the Datak chemicals up to about 4 months ago. The
ER-71 photoresist and ER-8 developer did a fine job for boards I made
including some millimeter-wave circuits on 5 mil thick Duroid that required
tolerances to a couple thousandths of an inch.

However, either the quality control of Datak photoresist has gone to hell or
they changed it, because I've had a lot of trouble getting even coats of it
on the boards with the newer ER-71. I asked them about it with no replies.

I've gotten lots of G-10/FR-4 board material of all sizes and thicknesses at
hamfests. It's usually pretty cheap there. A couple years ago I got some
2-ft square pieces of .062 inch thick G-10, double-sided copper, for $3
apiece.

You can cut grooves in the copper with an X-Acto knife, making individual
pads on the board that work quite well. I've done that too.

Zack
