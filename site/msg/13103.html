I would know by the content.  As a ham radio operator, I could identify 
anything said as being from that source. (Frank S is also a ham.) CB 
transmissions would also be easy to identify by the content.

And when I ask a question like "Are there others here with you?" and get 
an answer like "You bet," or if I ask "Are you happy?" and get a response 
"Course I'm happy" (actual EVP's I've gotten), it's a pretty good chance 
those are exact answers to the questions I asked.

As for the radio station, it was a continuous recording on mine.  I could 
hear the DJ talking, the intros to the music he gave, the music, the 
connercials, everything.  I doubt you are only going to get a single word 
or few words from a radio station close by if it is getting into your 
unit. And I suspect a ghost isn't going to be doing an ad for the local 
car dealer!

Also, I didn't mention in my last post that I tried the experiment with my 
recorder within a couple blocks and then at increasing distances from the 
radio station towers. At a quarter mile away or more I couldn't hear it in 
the recording.  I also tried the local police and some microwave towers, 
right under the towers, and heard nothing from those sources. But they 
are on much higher frequencies than AM or FM radio and are not known to 
get into any electronics around here.
