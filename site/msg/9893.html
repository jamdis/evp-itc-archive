Stefan Bion wrote:
Hi all,

a phonetic analysis of the "SpeakJet allophone EVP" might be interesting as well in order to find out which allophones are combined to form an EVP, and to verify if we heard correctly. As you perhaps already know from the manual, EVPmaker can record "EVP sessions", and log the labels of the used cues along with each EVP:

http://www.stefanbion.de/evpmaker/evpmkr_e.htm#abschn38

In my initial experiments using the SJ allophones with EVPmaker of March 26, I used the "EVP session" functionality rather than a microphone to record the session, so my own voice wasn't recorded. Instead, the labels of the allophones, which are included in the cue list of the SJ-Allophones.wav file, were logged in the session. See screenshots for EVPmaker settings to work with "EVP sessions":

http://tech.groups.yahoo.com/group/EVP-ITC/files/Stefan/Phonetic%20analysis%20of%20SpeakJet%20/ScreenshotsEVPmakerSession.png

I have examined three of the EVPs from my March 26 session and noted the allophones they are composed of:

Example 1
---------

EVP: "I little sir help you with it" (or "eclipse")

Allophones: OHIH NGE BE PE UH SO AXRR AYWW PE IHWW BE ZH IH KO TS

Transcription: OHIH = I, NGE BE PE UH = little, SO AXRR = sir, AYWW PE = help, IHWW = you, BE ZH IH KO TS = eclipse / with it

Link: http://tech.groups.yahoo.com/group/EVP-ITC/files/Stefan/Phonetic%20analysis%20of%20SpeakJet%20/20080326_021629-010%20-%20I%20little%20sir%20help%20you%20with%20it.wav

Example 2
---------

EVP: "That's the help of air" (or "err")

Allophones: TH DO WH SE BO KO AYWW HE ZZ OW VV NGO EYRR

Transcription: TH DO WH SE = that's, BO = the, KO AYWW HE = help, OW VV = of, EYRR = air

Link: http://tech.groups.yahoo.com/group/EVP-ITC/files/Stefan/Phonetic%20analysis%20of%20SpeakJet%20/20080326_222218-002%20-%20that%27s%20the%20help%20of%20air%20%28err%29.wav

Example 3
---------

EVP: "Eight air apples for them"

Allophones: EYIY TU TH EYRR EH HE LE SE NGO WW AX MM

Transcription: EYIY TU TH = eight, EYRR = air, EH HE LE SE = apples, NGO WW = for, AX MM = them

Link: http://tech.groups.yahoo.com/group/EVP-ITC/files/Stefan/Phonetic%20analysis%20of%20SpeakJet%20/20080326_222218-004%20-%20eight%20air%20apples%20for%20them.wav

http://tech.groups.yahoo.com/group/EVP-ITC/files/Stefan/Phonetic%20analysis%20of%20SpeakJet%20/ScreenshotAuditionCuelist.png

You can also select and play more than one allophone by dragging the selection while pressing the left mouse button, and then click play.
