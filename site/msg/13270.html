This might be helpful when creating your test, or perhaps in the future:

A little while back, I sent out a link to someone's evp that they
thought was very clear (I think it was one of Coletta's).  It wasn't a
very well controlled test I was doing, since there were comments
associated with the youtube sound that I asked for people not to
read,but of course, couldn't be sure they didn't.)

Anyway, the results I got back were:
2 people from this yahoo group thought they heard what Mike heard
(though both posted their results to the group, so influence there
from one to the other should be considered).

4 other people heard variously "Who are you?", "Whoosh whoosh whoosh",
"the green grass", and nothing.

The problem I found also is that 3 people said they really didn't
think there were any words, but if they kept listening, they could
tell me what it sounded most like.

I have a door at home that squeaks when I open it.  It sounds like
"Eeagle".  I'm pretty sure it's not a spirit inhabiting the door, and
that my 10W40 oil is not performing an exorcism.  The point is, when
listening to EVPs, some people in my test said they thought they heard
words.  And some said they didn't think so, but if they had to pick
something it sounded most like, it was these words.  The latter is the
"Squeaky door" effect I described.

So I think it would be a good idea to include the collection of that
in the results.  People could be asked on a scale of 1-5, how certain
were you that you heard words in that sound (1=no words, 2=fairly
doubtful, 3=not sure either way, 4=fairly sure, 5=very certain) What
words if any?

That might help to not only get people's assessment of the sound, but
also allow them to express the strength of their conviction, which
seemed an important component of several of the comments I was getting.
