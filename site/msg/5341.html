Dear all,

From my attempts at making a quick study of EVP-ITC via use of boxes 
in reading your extremely informative posts and listening to posted 
files, an obvious question arises (please indulge my technical 
ignorance here):  I understand these boxes work on scanning radio 
frequencies and that boxes have been created to scan AM, FM, and 
shortwave.  What about other frequencies, e.g., walkie-talkie 
channels (public, private and government), television broadcast 
channels, and all the in-between frequencies?  In other words, while 
scanning, for example, an AM channel, would it not be possible for 
the radio receiver to pick up another band, let's say, short-wave?  
The reason I'm asking is that years ago I was dictating something 
into the hand-held mic of a cheap cassette tape recorder and imagine 
my surprise when in playing back what I've just dictated, instead of 
hearing just my voice, I also heard in the background a two-way 
conversation between truckers using their CB radios that my cheap mic 
had picked up while I was recording!

My point is even if we're scanning only certain frequencies, can't 
other frequencies bleed through and if so, perhaps this may be the 
reason for some of the non-sequitor responses picked by the boxes?

Another question:  Astronomers use scanning devices to pick up 
signals from outer space -- are their receivors constructed on 
basically the same principles as the radio receivers in the ITC 
boxes?
