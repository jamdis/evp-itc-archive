For the techno droids out there--

If anyone out there is trying to build my circuits, there are a few 
components that I have identified now as critical for proper operation. They 
are the caps on the reference resistors on the gain stages for the white 
noise and the amp after the first low pass filter. The caps that go to the 
negative supply voltage, those have to be large, at least 100uf aluminum, or 
47uf tantalums, the tantalum caps are the best. With 10uf capacitors as 
shown on current vesions of the schematics, there is just not enough gain 
for the low frequency signals that are developed into the random tuning 
voltage. Even using 10uf tantalum capacitors is not enough. I'm making a 
board now with all tantalum capacitors. I get my tantalum caps from old hard 
drives, and other computer related boards, so I can afford to experiment. 
Use at least 20 volt rated capacitors.
