> It depends on what radiation you're talking about,  the electronic
> equipment and what it's made of.
>
> EMP (electromagnetic pulse) generated at the time of a nuclear detonation
> will probably wipe out any solid-state electronics that isn't heavily
> shielded against it. Alpha and beta particles could affect some electronics
> that managed to survive an EMP.
>
> The older stuff like RTL and TTL logic is probably not going to be much
> affected by the alpha and beta radiation. CMOS devices, sensitive to static
> discharges, would be next. VLSI chips have the greatest chance of being
> affected (read: home computers). Vacuum tube circuits would hardly be
> affected at all, even by EMP.
> Strange that some of our oldest devices would be the last to go.
> :-)
