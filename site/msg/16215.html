Please note that I used the word 'evidence' not proof. We are working here in what is best called an 'edge' or 'border' science. This is the province of theoretical constructs, frameworks and models. It is not applied science. The sort of proofs implied in the response below are meaningful in applied research, such as in the physical sciences. Unfortunately, this research has not yet advanced to that stage. And for that reason, it is premature to suggest that such proofs should be expected at this juncture. 

We use terms like 'suggests' and 'implies' precisely for this reason. Nothing is known for certain, and it would be (and is) irresponsible to suggest to the contrary at this point. 

What we seek in our models is consistency with observations. What I have done here is to provide reports of observations which suggest a coherent link that in turn provides one possible explanation to the phenomena in question.

I would suggest that a "proof" might be had if anyone could describe a condition wherein no belief per se were required and the only explanation of a phenomenon were due to discarnate activity. This means that no alternative explanation is allowable. Hundreds of quite brilliant people have tried for well over a century to find this one thing and thus far, have been unsuccessful.

>     Let's examine the supporting evidence a bit more closely. First consider the potential link between healing and PK. Both phenomena can occur due to a local or distant agent. Absent healing for example would be the distant analogy to distank or remote PK. Both have been studied in controlled experiments. Dr Larry Dossy in Texas is one of the primary investigators in healing research. Both healing and general PK are reliably observed to be either intentional or spontaneous. So both exhibit similar traits. Both involve apparent manipulation by the mind of the physical environment. And certainly healing strongly implicates an indirect effect on complex systems. So it makes some sense that the two may indeed be related.
> 
>   Now let's turn to the evidence for PK as an explanation for the EVP. A close colleague of mine, a respected parapsychologist in Los Angeles, was working years ago in a well-known haunted site in the Hollywood area. He recorded the voice of the home's owner, while standing next to the man. During this interval the man did not speak; no one heard what was recorded until it was played back. This sounds like a classic EVP thus far. The playback was easily heard by all. The man recognized his own voice, including swear words; he had a penchant for swearing in his normal speech. This anecdotal story would suggest that PK could be an explanation for EVPs based on this single example alone. 
> 
>   Lastly we'll consider a recent experiment by Dr. Dean Radin. In this experiment, mediators were asked to influence which paths photons would take passing through one of two very tiny slits. You would expect this to occur normally by random chance, producing an equal distribution of photons in two regions on the target photograph. When uninfluenced, this is exactly what happened. When the mediator tried to 'block' one of the slits, the distribution shifted, indicating success. But this is not what's important - it is necessary background. For the next trial, the machine was turned on and the exposure taken with no influence attempted. In fact, the mediatator was not even present. The following day the mediatator was asked to influence the machine. He was not told the machine was turned off and could not even record at that time. The next day the image was inspected for the first time - the meditator again was not present. The image revealed a pattern similar to the one produced when the mediatator successfully "blocked" the slit in the first trial. Theoretical physicst Dr. Richard Stroup suggested this indicated to him a demonstration of what he calls "retrocausation" - a notion in theoretical physics that the future actually impacts on the past (as well as the past impacting the future as most people believe). Dr. Radin raised an interesting point with respect to designing experiments. He now wonders if investigators are so entangled with experiments that they unwittingly can influence outcomes even before the experiment is designed. Remember, these are trained academicians at the top of their respective fields.
