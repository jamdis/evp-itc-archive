Mike, In all my sessions I try to ask at least two or three tech questions about the advancement of the equipment or any instructions or advice they have on furthering the research. I have even dedicated whole sessions to only technical and research questions, and have done whole sessions for scientist's that where trying to develop new technology. And those consisted of detailed tech questions concerning frequencies and tone and wave settings and or levels. In all those sessions and 100's more regular sessions that contained various research advancement questions. I have only received mostly vague answers and a couple of full precise answers of a purely technical nature, but no follow up answers or communication to build on those answers. You have to remember that the equipment we have been using is not really capable of spirits being able to send detailed lengthy communication that would be necessary to explain any technical advancement instructions or research answers. Our boxes up to this point have allowed mostly two to three word communication at a time and on rare occasions a sentence or phase consisting of more than 5 words. I have not yet ventured these tech or research questions with EVP maker or sound soap. But I am starting to be convinced that this is our first and best hope for solid length full communication capable of answering detailed questions or giving detailed instruction. Also I believe that our friends are holding back information, like a parent using flash cards with a child. If the child hits a snag the parent will assist with small hints until the child reaches the correct answer, but never answers for the child. This in turn assures complete understanding by the child of the problem and solution and also serves to teach reasoning and problem solving skills. I think they will help, but would like us to use their "Hints" to help ourselves. I will try to get answers to some of these questions using the "Allophone method" during my upcoming sessions. Hi all,

just a general comment on editing EVP: For scientific reasons, I would
suggest not to edit an EVP message partially, but only as a whole, in
order to rule out any possible suspicion of manipulation. You can for
instance increase the volume or dynamics of an entire clip (including
the experimenter' s voice), filter or denoise it to make it clearer, but
not cut out individual pieces (supposed "garbage") to make an EVP match
with what it "should be". At least, the unedited version should always
be provided along with the edited version.

But that's only my personal opinion which I thought I had to express, as
we should always be able to present our results to a critical public.

Stefan
