In the history of EVP/ITC, it has been acknowledged by some 
researchers that the first people to have recorded "voice phenomena" 
via magnetic means (i.e. a magnetophone) were the Catholic priests 
Fathers Gemilli and Ernetti back in 1952. (Gemelli received a message 
from his departed father while he and Ernetti were working with 
Gregorian chants). This communication bothered them so they had an 
audience with Pope Pius XII who was quoted as saying: "Dear Father 
Gemelli, you really need not worry about this. The existence of this 
voice is strictly a scientific fact and has nothing whatsoever to do 
with spiritism. The recorder is totally objective. It receives and 
records only sound waves from wherever they come. This experiment may 
perhaps become the cornerstone for a building for scientific studies 
which will strengthen people's faith in a hereafter."

In addition - Friedrich Jurgenson was a very well-known figure to 
both Pope Pius XII (who commissioned Jurgenson to paint numerous 
portraits of him and allowed Jurgenson to do archeologic explorations 
of the ruins of Pompeii) and to Pope Paul VI (who also commissioned 
Jurgenson to paint portraits of him and to produce a documentary for 
him which resulted in Jurgenson receiving the Order of Commendatore 
Gregorio Magno). It is alleged that Jurgenson discussed his "voice 
phenomena" recordings with Pope Paul and that the pope never spoke of 
reservations about the technique but I haven't found any definite 
proof of those conversations. You would think, though, that Pope Paul 
had to have known about Jurgenson's "voice phenomena" work either 
from first-hand talks to Jurgenson or indirectly via Vatican staff 
members and would have spoken to Jurgenson about any reservations he 
had. Note that Jurgenson's recording work occurred from late 1959 
until his death in 1987.
