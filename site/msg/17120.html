The tuner is *the most critical component.*  I have not been able to build
usable home made tuners, the problem being they are extrmemly noisy when
swept.  This problem prevents me from any serious production. I do make a
TRF tuner based on the MK484 chip, and that type of reciever eleiminates
almost all the static normally found on the AM band, but it doesn't have the
selectivity of super hets, so broadcast fragments tend to be longer,
with the "voices" seemingly in between the fragments. With a fast sweep
though, at times they will use all the audio and modualate a voice into it.

 I use digital radios I pick up at Thrift strores, as well as the tuners
from the Radio Shack radios.

 The sweep is little more than the triangle function from a funtion
generator, I add a DC offset and amp so I can cover the entire AM and FM
bands of a given tuner.  I do use a random sweep, but so far that is a real
pain in the butt to make it run consistently, but I think I have a solution.


 For store bought tuners you have to use a sope or meter and find all the
powere=, band change and tuning voltage inputs before removing it from the
main unit. After removing the tuner test sweep it with a 9 volt battery and
100k pot to see how noisy it is as it's swept, and to determine the tuning
voltage range.
