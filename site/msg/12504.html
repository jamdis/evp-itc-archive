"A perfect example (which I believe I mentioned before) is Satan, 
which was a Hebrew word appropriated by the Church under the context 
of "the devil" himself or "lucifer". In Judaism it refers to 
god's "angel" that's translated as the "adversary" - it stands 
for "choice". A "prosecutor" if you will, he gives you choice - to

choose against, there'd be no "free will", hence "Satan" or "ha-
Satan" was created. There's nothing inherently negative about that, 
other than the ominous notion to choose wisely.  All 
energy, "good", "bad", all comes from the same source in 
Jewish/Kabbalistic belief. "Angels", "demons", etc. are not
considered to have free will (that's what we were created for), and
are seen as simply direct extensions of the same "one" (hence the main
angels have "el" - alpeh lamed - as part of their name). Its based
via the concept of a pure monotheism viewpoint. All are simply
extensions and therefore parts of the whole."
