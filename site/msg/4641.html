I think there is a huge difference between the evidence obtained by  
EVP research and the Luminator pictures.  When you have a clear  
answer to a question that is captured in realtime, with witness  
potential and in a format that can be analyzed afterward, you have a  
heck of a lot of more leg to stand on, HMO.

I have tried telling people who claim their loved ones appeared in a  
Luminator picture, and I got exactly the emotive response I suppose I  
deserved for trying. You can reason with a broken heart, and that's  
what a lot of people have when they are photographed. I once attended  
a seance where a supposed medium held a flashlight up to his face at  
different angles in a dark room and claimed that viewers would see  
their relatives and other spirits manifest themselves over his face.  
And they did. A whole room of them shouted out that it was their  
relative or Abraham Lincoln, or whatever. Those of us who saw a guy  
in a dark room making faces with a spooky light on his face were told  
in no uncertain terms that we were skeptics and debunkers or that we  
didn't see the faces because we weren't rarified enough to do so.

As for the three Luminators allowing the guy's head to pass through  
the wall... Well, I'd have to see it, or better yet, personally  
experience it, to believe it myself.

As always, your milage may vary,

Ann
