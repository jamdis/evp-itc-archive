> ---- begin quote ----
> Like EVPmaker, the swept receiver method is capable of producing EVP,
> but it is also even more capable of producing false results. Some years
> ago, I did a series of experiments with EVPmaker.... What EVPmaker does
> is take a few seconds of speech, chop it into little pieces called
> phonemes and then randomize these phonemes. If you are lucky, you will
> find EVP.
> 
> What some people do, frustrated at not getting very much, is they make
> the slices wider, so then what you are getting are actually sizeable
> little chunks of the sentence. Actual words are still there. In that
> case you seem to be getting more EVP, but what you are getting is simply
> a rearranged sentence.
> ---- end quote ----
> 
> My comment on this:
> 
> If only phonemes are used, as is the case for the SpeakJet allophones
> file, this won't happen, since this source material definitely doesn't
> already contain speech. In order to rule out doubts as to getting "false
> EVPs", only audio sources should be used in the future which aren't
> already finished speech. So perhaps using SJ allophones are one step
> towards the right direction. Interesting, that this comes up right now...
