This is basically the "buzz stick" setup from lessemf.com Any suction 
cup type mic pickup and amplified speaker can work in a similar fashion 
as it is basically a single axis emf probe. I suppose according to the 
traditional evp coil induction theory that it could work, but about 4 
years ago I did extensive testing with this type of pickup on a yamaha 
4 track recorder with diodes and microphones on other tracks. The only 
success I had was with the tracks that had a regular microphone 
attached. As I understand it, this device transforms the em frequency 
to it's corresponsing audible frequency, sortof a "reverse mic".

I have never had any success in it's use, but it certainly is a cheap 
way to experiment. I don't see a connection to a Frank's box by 
functionality, Frank's boxes and similar model ghost boxes are much 
more complex and sophisticated. You can think of the above as an 
audible emf sensor.
