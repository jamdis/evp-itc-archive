Well, I finally got my own ghost box put together in a single, portable package.
I call it "Tom's Toob" (Tube).

Its a 25 inch long, 2"/id peice of PVC pipe, painted black. It has two slots
cut into its right half, near the center just big enough to accomidate the lower
half of a hacked 587 radio in one and an Avon "SpaFinder" sleep noise generator
in the other. The loudspeakers of both thus open inside the tube while the
controls remain outside the pipe and are easily accessible. I must say that
the resonance and sound mixing in the tube are great. The noise generator has
several different random sounds that might be useful, like a fairly clean "white
noise", a "water fall" sound and a "running stream" sound with a volume control.
Other sounds like "wind chimes" "jungle", etc. likely are less useful, but might
be fun to play with.

It seems to work, but I miss a lot---I need more practice, I suppose. 
"Bradley", a deceased friend of mine said his name-- and It did sound like his
voice. When I asked how he died--he replied "accident!"--which is true--he
drove his car under a moving train.--he said more, but I couldnt make it
out---gotta get the tape recorder running.

The picture of Tom's Toob should be in my album. My omly problem is that I
might need to speed the scan up just a bit. It there a capacitor or resistor I
can add to do this?

I like the fact that the pipe is easy to carry in one hand or on my shoulder. I
was inspired to use a pipe to house the components by a recent episode of "Ghost
adventures"--they only had a radio in theirs though.
