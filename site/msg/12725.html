Unfortunately most of my writing is white papers and technical reports, so I tend to write dryly. I also write several hundred e- mails a day, as I field a lot of questions about the research we are doing at the SPIRIT lab. So If I have a perceived tone, it is unintentional. My approach to the whole EVP/ITC issue is strictly common sense/scientific. I well know that most people are not doing what I am doing, and I don't expect them to. What I do is post what we do, and let other people duplicate it if they wish. By following strict testing protocols, we are able to seperate the wheat from the chauff so to speak. The thing is, people are entitled to their beliefs, at least so far. I am not so much interested in beliefs, but in finding answers. So I admit my approach is somewhat different. But the by product of my approach is one day I may be able to say you can communicate with the dead, and this is why, and produce the viable evidence. While many of you don't understand my motivations, what that may lead to is a branch of science, grants for research, and new discoveries that apply to many aspects of science. Or not. The important thing is to study the validity, probability, and source. I mean, what if ITC doesn't work, and the effects you are experienceing are due to something completely different? Proof is required to answer questions, and that is all I try to do.
