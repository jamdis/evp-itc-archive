Hi Al,

Do you mean the 14 MHz (20 meter) band or 21 MHz (15 meter) band? 14 
meters was never allocated to radio amateurs. :-)

Until we get some more sunspots, 15 meters and above has been fairly dead 
for over a year. 20 meters has quite a bit of activity and 40 and 80 
meters get most of the activity these days.

1485 kHz is in the AM broadcast band but isn't a standard channel in the 
USA, which goes in 10 kHz steps. There would be stations on 1480 and 1490 
in the USA, not necesssarily in your area, though. If you go to this page:

http://www.fcc.gov/mb/audio/amq.html

Enter 1480 as the lower frequency and 1490 as the upper frequency and 
you'll get a page listing all the stations on those two frequencies.

Zack
