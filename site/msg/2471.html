------=_NextPart_000_00FE_01C80F1D.4D76D200 Content-Type: text/plain; charset="iso-8859-1" Content-Transfer-Encoding: 7bit This is my first post in this group, so hello to everyone here. Frank's post prompted me to reply since I understand the frustration involved in paranormal investigations. It seems that every time we think we have something figured out, the rules change - so back to square one again. I have been involved in paranormal studies from childhood, part of which came from living in a house which was a civil-war era funeral parlor.Over the years I've studied most aspects of the strange, from ghosts to UFOs to Bigfoot, to... well, you get the idea. The last few years I've been involved particularly with ghosts and have been studying the methods of spirit communications closely. I am semi-retired now and have plenty of time for experimentation. My educational background is in Geology, Computer Science and Electrical Engineering. I spent many years designing analog sensors and other devices for well logging and oilfield instrumentation. Which brings me to what I wanted to say to Frank... Don't give up. Take a break from it for a while, and be less concerned what others think. If you have high standards, then your work will prove itself. There are always going to be naysayers and backbiters and they will never be satisfied with what you produce, so ignore them, and work with those who have open minds and the ability to use critical analysis without being totally negative. I believe your approch has merit, and am working on duplicating one of your devices myself. The last couple of years I have been closely examining methods of monitoring VLF-ELF frequencies and demodulating audio from them. This is the world of 'Earth Sounds' and over the years I have had some interesting results, particularly of voices singing and shreds of speech. My NASA Inspire receiver was the starting point, but it was too broad banded and desparately needed 60Hz notch filtering so I have retired it and am now starting development from the ground up on a receiver targeted for 20 - 2000 Khz in the EM spectrum. There is more to this than just adapting a high-gain audio amplifier - a lot of filtration needs to be done and I'm eliminating the use of any local oscillators or mixers to eliminate possible overload products from the broadcast band. Like you, I have a few detractors who tell me I'm wasting my time but I also have a good research group (A.S.U.P.) who is totally supportive. If you have seen any of the articles in Haunted Times or other publications about highly modified K-II EMF detectors being used, I plead guilty. I have built and distributed about ten of them to various people and groups, and have had some very encouraging feedback. So stay with it Frank, don't be discouraged, keep studying, working and posting... Thanks for the bandwidth and perhaps we can all exchange some good ideas here.
