So you have stated that the person conducting this study was an EVP expert?  That leads me to question how the study was actually conducted.  Listening to the box is certainly not like listening to an EVP and I personally disagree vehemently with the people out there who call box communications EVP's!

In an EVP situation, you are listening closely for a voice to sound typically over the quiet.  A box session forces one to listen through the noise as opposed to the silence.

Were these sessions recorded and listened back too or was an analysis made strictly from real time?  I do sessions all the time and I am lucky if I can pick up 35% of what comes through in real time.

And finally, try not to be too dismayed.  An EVP 'expert' makes their bread and butter parading their EVP techniques.  I witnessed one such expert who we all have at least heard of create an EVP.  How?  The person in question scratched their thumb nail against the recorders mic to create a sound.  This person then played back the recorder to me and pitched it down to make what this person say what was heard sound more convincing.  

If I made my money parading my EVP ability, I would discredit your box too.  Why?  Because box users are the future of ITC and paranormal research.  It is relatively cheap, vastly available, and provides instant gratuity when operating.  Who wants to sit alone in the dark talking to silence when you can hear the responses in real time?  EVP is still a valid form of communication, but ghost boxes threaten an EVP experts way to make money.  Boxes are being used more and more, especially on the television shows and public hunts.  This hurts the revenue stream for EVP experts who used to be the ones getting invited to guest star on hunts for a fee; they are now being replaced by box users.

In short, box use can be discredited by every expert and skeptic in the field.   It doesn't matter.  Ghost box use now has its place in paranormal culture and it is not going to leave it anytime soon.  Being situated in a paranormal group that is lead by a para-celebrity has put me in the unique position of visiting other groups and working with the other para-celebrities in this field quite frequently.  I can assure you that ghost boxes aren't going anywhere because the majority of these people have at least a shack hack in their paranormal arsenal.  

So let this expert discredit to his or her's hearts' content.  When they publish their results, the results become public and they are on the hook for what they say at that point (which could bite them in the ass one day).  Their results will not hurt the work that you are doing today Frank, nor will what they say influence this forums core group in any way because we know the boxes work. 

Tim
