Hi Andy.  I know everyone doesn't feel as I do, but I too disregard everything that isn't 
relevant when I'm using any of the sweep methods.  As for any special practices, I find that 
the more clear I am with my intention for the session, usually the better results I get.  

Some of the criteria I use when deciding whether or not to accept content in a session as 
intentional communication is:

	The spoken phrase is directly relevant to my preceding question or comment.  In 
some cases it is immediate, just as it would be in normal conversation.
	The duration of the response spans at least one full cycle of the back/forth sweep, 
sometimes several.
	The words are spoken in a single tone of voice.
	The intonation and cadence are suggestive of that which is considered normal human 
speech pattern.
	The validation by a loved one recognizing the voice and words.  Though subjective, I 
find it persuasive.


I have examples of various methods I've tried and explanations of the clips posted in my 
folder (Margaret Downey) here on the boards if you'd like to hear some of my results.  I've 
done a lot of work with the EVPM/allophones combo.

Margaret
