This is a comparison of boards, the ones on the right are baisc system using 
a car tuner, the one on the right is same, except I used almost all tantalum 
capacitors. You'll notice that most of the "cans" standing up on the board 
on the right, are missing from the one on the left. The caps were replaced 
with the little flat surface mount tantalum capacitors used in computers. 
The tantalum caps are the tiny yellow blocks that you might be able to see 
on the left hand board.  I found an easy method of adapting the surface 
mount caps to perf board, allowing me to experiment with these smaller, 
better components.  BTW, these represent two weeks work. The board with the 
black heats sinks next to the counter is the charger to for the batteries. 
The other board that's belly up I'm pulling sockets from for re-use. Then, 
in the background are echo boxes, on top is an aluminum foil box, and the 
bottom one is a wave guide type box.
