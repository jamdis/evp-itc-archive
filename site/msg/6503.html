DXing refers to trying to hear distant stations on shortwave, AM and 
longwave frequencies, if you don't have an amateur radio license. 
There is a class of us referred to as "lowfers" who can also transmit in 
the ranges of 136, 160-180 and 500 kHz. We listen for each other's signals 
from far away places.

With an amateur license, DXing refers to trying to actually contact other 
amateurs in other countries. There are over 300 "countries" recognized to 
be contacted at this time, although some are not actually countries, but 
are locations far away from the countries that actually own them (e.g. 
Norfolk Island owned by Australia, Peter I island owned by Norway).

There are also FM and TV band DXers.  In the last post I sent, I didn't 
mention the E layer of the ionosphere.  The E layer is usually not present 
but appears during certain random times, usually in the spring.  We call 
it "sporadic-E".  During these times, it's possible to pick up FM and 
lower-band TV signals (channels 2 through 7) from hundreds of miles away.

I do a little bit or a lot of all of this! I've officially contacted over 
300 countries by amateur radio.
