Several of us in the past have gotten together to do sessions via the internet. I enjoy listening when others are contributing, noting the responses coming over, even though I have not asked the question. I like to write the "so called" answer pertaining to that specific question down and see if in fact the others heard the same response during that period of time. As you may have already discovered that even though you have preplanned to concentrate on one area of questioning, the "spirits" may just have something else on their agenda and go in a completely different direction (in which this is where your note session is an asset). When I turn any of the boxes on, I immediately start my recorder whether I am asking questions or not. I like to run the box now prior to an question and/or answer session, with out saying a word (like Frank does) just to see what they may be discussing at that time. You just might be amazed at the conversations they are having without acknowledging us. I have compared it to a gathering of friends gossiping over a cup of coffee and in this life time if you were to interrupt one of your friends just might find it offensive. Therefore, when they are finished, they will bring you into the conversation or identifying you by name. I just go with the flow, and with each session seems to be a new and exciting adventure for me! In addition I also have been exchanging my raw audio with others (mainly Joel) who in-turn seems to confirm or deny the clips that I believe I hear. I just recently video taped a session and upon reviewing my actual data I have come to discover that the video captured very clear audio in which I either missed or overlooked on my recorder. This has been my experience with conventional EVP and various recorders so I am not suprised that this would be any different. It does however add another venue and opportunity to get capture as much audio as possible. For you never know just what you could have missed. I wish everyone the best of luck with all their sessions and boxes, no matter what way to conduct your sessions, the results and possitive and most enjoyable to listen too!
