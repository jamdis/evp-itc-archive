Misty Guy wrote: Frank has provided a paper with a block diagram, MOST (but not all) of the schematics, brief description of operation and a few color pics of the internal wiring. Look in the Files section of the group on the Yahoo groups website for "theghostbox.pdf". Unfortunately, he has left out some details (like size/composition of the "echo chamber", etc.) and most of the schematics are hard to read due to digitizing (resolution) problems. The key component that can be difficult to find is the Sanyo LA1135 AM Tuner IC that's at the heart of the system. Frank has apparently scrounged this chip from old car radios. Here's how it operates: The RVG (Random Voltage Generator) output is fed to the "tuning" input of the LA1135 AM radio receiver. What this basically does is similar to a human being rapidly and randomly hitting the tuning station buttons in a car radio or varying the tuning dial of a stationary or portable radio. The output of this randomly-tuned AM radio is then fed to a small speaker into an internal "echo chamber" (although his box also provides an output jack for an external speaker to use an external "echo chamber" or even just the room) which also contains a small electret microphone (these are the types that a very tiny and can be clipped to your lapel, tie, etc.). The output of this mike is then fed to another speaker (or optionally to headphones) for the output. Where the "entities" interact with the box is in this "echo chamber" (probably just a small, soundproof plastic box) using the speech snippets from the randomly-tuned AM radio stations as a carrier for any EVP. Hope this helps everyone!
