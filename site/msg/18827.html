It depends on how you listen to the box, the messages are layered a lot of times.  I do the research a little different than others.  I don't snip out full radio words and have my mind try and make sense of that word.  I use snippets of sound as the radio sweeps through, with that sound they are able to make words.  

The files aren;t as clear sounding, but they are authentic and harder to toss out as evidence.  That's why in my opinion a faster sweeping radio is the way to go.  They will use a fast sweep, you just have to get use to listening a little different.

If you ask the box "What's your name?", your brain will automatically be listening for a name on the radio.  Although it could happen that way, it's more likely a coincidence.  They call that chasing ghosts, that's why Frank doesn't ask questions.  I usually don't either, because i'm usually doing a sample recording of a new box.  The messages are still in there upon review of that sample audio.
