> Would I love to be able to
> hold real time 2 way communications with the other side? Of course.
> However, I do not think enough research into the possibilities has

Again I have to ask: what kind of research and by who?

There is nothing exotic about the electronics of the box itself. It  
can't *physically* hurt a person more than an a portable radio can--  
and yearly few people die or are injured at the hands of  their  
portable radios. What's left, and what it seems you're fearful of, is  
potential psychic injury by the box rather than plain old blunt-force  
trauma. I would submit that diagnosing psychic injury is not anymore  
possible than diagnosing Harmony. And what it comes down to is that  
the only people who can make up the body that researches and judges  
whether the box should be approved are the very people who are using  
it right now.

>
> Those of us who have been on countless investigations with many people
> will quickly admit that there are some people we'd rather not
> investigate with. When they are present, negative things tend to
> happen. We all know one or two people who we can say "felt dark" from
> the first meeting.

So, you move away from those people because you don't like their  
vibe. You drop them from the investigation team or join a new team or  
investigate on your own, don't you? But you don't stop investigating  
all together. Because you have a subjective feeling about someone is  
not evidence that they are "dark" and shouldn't be allowed to have a  
simple piece of electronic equipment.

> We all admit that being mentally sound is an
> important quality in this field. If you have mental issues, this may
> not be the area of research for you.

While I agree with out on this point, it is also wonderfully true of  
all careers, avocations, and hobbies, not just EVP research. If you  
are nuts, you are going to act nuts in whatever activity or process  
you're involved in. Unless your behavior crosses the line to  
criminal, the rest of us have to deal with you in the traditional  
away--avoidance. The only way that an out-of-Harmony type person can  
hurt anyone else with a box is to hit them over the head with it,  
electrocute them with it, scare them with it, or attempt somehow to  
gain sway over them with it. All that they can do to themselves by  
using it is hit themselves over the head with it, electrocute  
themselves with it, scare themselves with it, or use it to bolster  
whatever neuroses or psychopathies they have. And while that is  
unfortunate, it is also true of everything from revival meetings to  
household appliances.


> We don't bring children on
> investigations for obvious reasons. BUT! If you want a box that could
> possibly open up a portal and bridge the gap between here and the
> dark, sure! Anyone who wants one should have it!

Wait--how do you know that when you use the box it opens a  
"portal" (implying that something can come through into the physical  
space where you are located) versus simply communicate over an  
electronic device that tunes into frequencies that other phones  
can't? I've seen absolutely no evidence that anything can slither  
through open cassette, reel-to-reel, digital, or box-induced doorways  
to torment us. The person on the other end of a phone conversation,  
no matter how evil, cannot undulate his way through the fiber optics  
to axe murder us. We know that and we simply hang up and cut off the  
conversation. If he calls back, we hang up again. While sometimes we  
get EVPs that are rude, nasty, or mean, I think that 30 years of  
research has shown us that whoever said, "Screw you," on your tape  
recorder is not also capable of slashing your tires. All they can do  
is frighten you. That may be unpleasant, but it is this internal  
biochemical process of your own body that leaves most lingering  
effects, HMO.

>

The question that I have for you is have you considered the enormous  
amount of good that comes from use of the box or any EVP technique?  
For every person who scares herself silly there at least a dozen, in  
my opinion, who lose their fear of death. For every person who goes  
on a power trip because they can contact spirits, there are a dozen  
who share messages of hope all comers. The subjective evidence for  
positive outcome outweighs the subjective evidence for negative  
outcomes.
