The problem I was having was in the random pulse stage, the DC levels
would shift over time, and the dual supply, teh DC level of the randomly
varying low freq AC signal would shift to a point where it could not be
rectified. In the single supply version, same problem, the DC offset would
drift, producing the same problem. While this takes a few minutes for the
DC levels to drift down, the random tuning voltage comes on everytime. The
trick was to use a single supply AC amp un biased, and let the random AC
voltage input settle around ground, so it's self rectifying.
