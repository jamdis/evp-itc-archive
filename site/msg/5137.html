Note, you will not always hear stations in these bands. The 120 through 41 
meter bands are predominantly nighttime bands and you'll hear mostly 
static/noise during the day. The 49 and 41 meter bands usually start to 
open in the late afternoon.

The 31 meter band right now might have stations audible at most times 
except for very late at night.

25 meters and up are predominantly daytime bands.  Since we are at the 
solar cycle minimum, the higher frequencies (15 meters and up) will 
probably be dead most of the time. In another 4 years or so, when we reach 
the peak of the new solar cycle 24, these higher frequencies may have 
stations audible in them round the clock. The 11-year solar cycle causes 
higher frequencies to be fairly useless at the bottom (which is where we 
are now) and pushes the maximum usable frequency up to above 50 MHz at the 
cycle peaks.
