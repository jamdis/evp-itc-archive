Received and held my first session with Joe's Box yesterday, and 
thought it might be good to see what you guys think of what I got. I 
will upload them in a minute under Ursula- Joe's Box. 

The two clips are actually from the second session, which I did about 
five minutes after the first, uneventful one.

Both clips are from the same session.

The first clip is from the beginnng of the four-minute session.  I 
have just asked, "Is there something you want to say?" and then 
before and while I repeat the question, a voice says, "-the fuck up.  
Shut the--"

The second clip happens later.  I had asked "What is my name?" and 
the earlier ("shutthefuckup") smart-alecky voice had answered "Ursula 
Anne" in a singsong way (This is a little weird because my real name 
is Anne Ursula, so kids used to tease me by calling me Ursula Anne at 
school after I started going by my middle name at age 11.) 

The second clip starts immediately after, when the same voice 
says, "Zip It!"
Then, a second voice says, "Shhh. Listen, you'll scare her." 
Then the first voice says, "Aww!"  Then something I can't make out in 
short bursts.
Then, I say "I'm listening" because I heard "Listen" from the second 
voice.
After my voice you hear "Here you go!" and then "What's Up?!" from 
that second voice again.

I'm full of wonder about this box.  It was odd. People say the boxes 
FEEL different from anything else, and I really did.  I KNEW somehow 
when I started that second session that there would be something, and 
it was like the language opened itself up to me.  It's a very 
different sounding sort of thing from anything else.  I can't really 
describe it.  It's much, much different from anything I've done 
before and quite Awe-some in a literal sense.

I was also very surprised that I wasn't at all afraid of whatever 
idiot was swearing and saying stupid things.  Like a bully on a 
playground, really.  Nothing scarier than that.  

Thanks, Steve, for giving me the chance to experience this tool.  And 
thanks, Frank, dear Frank who I've never met, for having the box idea 
in the first place.

Ursula
