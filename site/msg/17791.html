I missed the original post on the PCB, or couldn't find it. Anyway, I use a company called ExpressPCB  http://www.expresspcb.com/ $60 for 3 their of mini boards. I can fit all functions minus the tuner on a mini board. I use SMT with the mini boards, and theat's not difficult using 1206 components. mouser carries all the SMT parts.

 But mostly I use perf board and use the money I would have spent on PCBs for parts. 

 I did find a schematic for the function generator kit others have been using for sweep, and converted to use the LM324, 1N4148 diodes and added a DC offset and amp. I made a board to see how the sine wave functions as sweep instead of the triangle wave.

> > Yes, that board should work. If you have a single-sided board pattern, after
> > you've developed the photoresist (or ironed on the toner, if you do it that
> > way) spray the other side of the board with spray paint and let dry. I like
> > to use the clear coat paint but it doesn't really matter.
> > 
> > After you've etched one side, clean the paint and the photoresist off (clean
> > both sides) with acetone, MEK, etc. Do this in a well-ventilated area! Then
> > drill your holes.
> > 
> > If you are going to make a double-sided board, drill a few of the holes that
> > have common pads on both sides to orient the pattern on the other side and
> > repeat whatever method you use to put a resist on that side. After it's
> > developed/ironed on, spray the already etched side with paint and etch the
> > other side. Clean off the board and drill holes.
