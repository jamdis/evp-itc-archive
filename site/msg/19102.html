--- In EVP-ITC@yahoogroups.com, Tim Woolworth <tjwoolworth@...> wrote:
>
> I think Frank said it best when he said it gets tedious with longer
> sessions.  As you do more sessions, you will want to continue conversing
> with the Others for as long as possible.  But, upon playback, careful
> analysis (with headphones and Adobe Audition) takes me an hour for every 5
> minutes.  So three sessions a week at 15 minutes a session can eat up a lot
> of time.  So I try to keep my home sessions to 10 minutes or less.  There
> are times when they are not too communicative and I keep pressing on past 15
> minutes and I always dread it when I do because there is so much more to
> listen to.
> 
> In the field, it is a different story.  Session times can go from 15 to 40
> minutes and leave you with a helluva lot of audio to consider.  Being very
> active in investigations and home sessions can make going over the audio a
> very dreadful process if you inundate yourself with longer sessions.
> 
> So my advice?  Keep it under ten unless you are getting something fantastic.
>  Also, keep a word file on every session file that documents the time and
> communication received in the session for future reference.  Sometimes
> people and messages come across that I got over a year ago and I would have
> never known it if it weren't for the files I keep.
> 
> Hope this helps.
