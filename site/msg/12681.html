He is coming at it from a scientific POV. His stance is based on the lack of scientific evidence, controlled experiments and claims that can easily be duplicated. This is why ITC is scoffed by the scientific community. Making extraordinary claims without scientific evidence to back it up makes every EVP researcher look like a cool aid drinker. Mr. Moon originally indicated that only a magic thirty people could make the device work, which would suggest selective physics. Physics works the same for everyone, and any real proof must have scientific principles backing it up. He is open to there being something to it. It just hasn't stood up yet to serious scientific scrutiny. So far it has failed at every turn. Spiricom was a fraud. The interesting part of the story is why it was a fraud. It reveals things about the individuals psychology that gives a great deal of insight about perceptions and ego. We want to believe it works, and often times it makes us fall victim to pareidolia. Case in point. Take a recording of a voice produced by any one of these devices. Play it in isolation to five people. Have them write down what they hear. If they all hear the same thing alone, then there is something to it. If they all hear something different, it is Gaussian noise and pareidolia, a well documented effect. This is why serious, disciplined research is tantamount to this part of the field ever being taken seriously. David M.
