I ran a bunch of short sessions tonight (about 20 seconds each) asking a question, 
listening for the answer, then going onto the next based on what I heard previously. Most 
of these answers came very quickly. I have removed all of the non-relevant sound between 
Q&A, but very little has been modified in the answering phrase itself (just made them so it 
didn't sound like a stutter.)  Parts 4, 5, 6, and 7 were from a single session, because I was 
hearing the answer real-time, but not understanding it.  I thought Arthur was trying to tell 
me specifically what kid of pet.  Likewise, the last 2 parts were together in one session.

I have lowered my voice to hopefully make the responses more clear. I'm working to 
establish whether or not there is any indication of probable intentional communication 
taking place. So far, I'd say there are very strong indicators.  

Can you say A.B.C.?
"A.B.C.D.E."  (this answer is sort of buried in the main sound)

How about Tom Butler?
"Tom Butler."

Do you know the name of either of Tom Butler's cats?
"(Sh)asta's their cat." (the "Sh" sound is weak or missing)

Do you have any pets?
"I have a pet."

I heard you say I have a...?
"Pet."

You have a what?
"Pet."

What kind of pet do you have?
"An animal." (LOL- ask a silly question, get a silly answer!)

I understand you have a pet animal, but what is the species?
"A purple deer."

OK, I could have sworn you said you have a purple deer.
"I told you purple deer." 

OK, I'm gonna close for the evening.  Can you tell me goodbye?
"I love you."

I heard you say I love you.  Love you too.
"Thank you."

The speed for this session was set on 106%, which is a little too fast for listening on 
playback, but I get good responses with the setting.  I have slowed the tempo on the 
answers by -18 in order to make them just a little clearer.  :-)
