You can create a wave in anything. You can create a wave in a rope by having
a person hold each end and then having one person wiggle the rope. You can
create a wave in a lake by dropping a rock into it. The thing that carries
the wave is the propagating medium. In these examples it's the rope and
water respectively.

In the case of sound, the propagating medium is the air. It is a
longitudinal pressure wave. It cannot be picked up on a receiver that is
capable of receiving the same frequencies as electromagnetic waves.

There are receivers and antennas that can pick up electromagnetic waves of
the same frequencies as sound waves. But in this case, the propagating
medium is the aether. They are transverse electromagnetic waves and you
can't hear them.
The electromagnetic spectrum extends from sub-audible frequencies all the
way up to the frequencies of X-rays and cosmic rays. Different methods exist
to detect or receive different parts of this spectrum. I'm involved in a ULF
group that specializes in receiving electromagnetic waves of the Schumann
resonances which are around 6 Hz, plus or minus.
