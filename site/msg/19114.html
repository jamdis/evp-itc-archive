Its a crystal radio receiver. Probably made in the early 20's judging by the condition and parts used. The front controls are tuning and headphone volume.
A crystal detector is meant to plug into that socket, and the set wont work without it. A (modern) germanium diode could be used instead and soldered across the socket terminals.
The wire on the coil looks like its loosing its insulation so the coil is probably faulty and would have to be re-wound.
