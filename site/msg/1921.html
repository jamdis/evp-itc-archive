Thanks Joe! I have a bar antenna I'm using now, and I think the main problem 
is I'm trying to use too large of tuning cap, maybe the the varactor is even 
bad(?). Trying to resonate the antenna with the varactor is like feeding a 
short, I get nothing. So far I have to put the bar antenna across the choke, 
showin in the schematic for the LA1816. The choke provieds a DC path for the 
RF Vcc, I get nothing without it, but just putting a finger on the choke 
results in good loud signals.

The bar has 30 turns #34 that is across the choke, and another 120 turns 
like a primary, with one end grounded, and the other end just goes to a 
longer wire. Just connecting the large end across the input results in less 
signal. I got good clear signals late last night with this. The clarity of 
some of the voices even surprises me.

Then there's problems with a bad connection on the main board, and if I push 
on the board, the audio clears up. I think I have it working well enough to 
show the PCB design will work ok, so I can order the PCBs, which will 
eliminate some of the problems. I added extra "holes" so there's alternate 
antenna connection possiblities, while leaving the original RF amp idea in 
place.

I do have antenna rods of various sizes in the junk box.

Like I said, I ain't no engineer!

>Hi Frank,
>
>I wonder if you have the small bar antenna I suggested awhile ago? That
>antenna has two coils and can be used in any way two windings on the same
>coil can be used. These antennae are resonated with parallel caps. I've
>resonated two windings in series with a parallel varactor diode to get a
>high impedance tank circuit. Total series inductance 600uH, in parallel 
>with
>100pf diode. The larger coil (500uH) can be resonated with 140pf tuning 
>cap.
>These antennae are used without input windings because they rely on the
>concentration of flux in the ferrite core to gather radio signal.
>
>The long replacement rod antennas have two windings, one for AM MW
>(broadcast) and a smaller one for SW. These windings are separately
>resonated with  tuning caps on there respective bands.
>
>These antennae are not viewed as input transformers, but as signal 
>gathering
>tank circuits. However, I believe they can also act as broadband magnetic
>loop antennae. In this mode they are not sharply tuned to each channel, but
>are meant to develop a broadband output. The characteristic impedance is
>high compared to the tuner input and might need matching for good results.
>
>I suggest the use of a grounded foil shield to reduce static pickup.
>
