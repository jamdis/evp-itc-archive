If you use the linear sweep, the circuit is very simple. Just op amps. It seems you have to use the J-FET input amps, like TL074. I tried using an LM324, but it was just too noisy. Using and older digital car tuner module can simlify consturction, requiring only a simple amplifier to drive a speaker. I use the LM386 chips f audio amps. in the sweep section I use the quad op amp. Three of the channels are used for the sweept generator, and the fourth is the mic amp. I think the random mode is a pain in the butt to make work, and at least to me, has no advantage. This is the main schematic, the rest is mostly audio amplifiers, and just depends on what you have, and what you can get. You could even feed the tuner audio directly to a boom box, or other external amplifier/recorder. Contrary to what some say, none of this stuff is written in stone. I have a "Mini Box" that uses an older digital Walkman board, the linear sweep circuit, and a couple audio amps to drive the output speakers. It's just a matter of getting the concept, and making something that fits it. I am increasingly reluctant to provide this stuff for people that think it's their god assigned mission or some damn thing. There is no one, without exception that will use the box as simply a means to hear from friends, spirits, and varous entities--personal communications! Always it has to be treated like some stupid oracle granting saintliness to the user. For me is only a source of guidence, not much more than an electronic Tarot deck, or quija board.
