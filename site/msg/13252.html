I know what you mean Misty - it does seem that interpretations are often varied.  I am thinking that the best way to make progress is to get an audio sample that you think is most representative of a given approach's effectiveness, and send that privately to several people.  Name the file something innocuous (like File1) and don';t tell the people you send it to what you think you hear.  Also, ask the people not to discuss the file with anyone else, and have them send what they think they hear directly back to you,rather than posting the results in a forum or group.

I think that kind of test is helpful, since it seems to me that once someone tells me what the audio clip sounds like, then it's hard for me to hear anything else in it.  But,that could be influencing my interpretation (like someone pointing to a cloud, telling me its a horse, and then I have trouble NOT seeing a horse) ,so it's better to listen before it is suggested to me what i should be hearing.

This way, I think the most progress can be made, because if people just keep confirming what we think we hear, and it turns out it's just noise that we are forcing an interpretation into, we won't be making progress (though it might make us feel good to believe that we're being successful).  Getting negative feedback to one's approach is not as fun, and might feel like some non-believer is trying to debunk us.  But, really, that can be the best feedback for progress, since it helps us to change tactics when our current approach is not effective, rather than continuing down an ineffective path, getting accolades from friends, but missing out on other paths that might show more promise,.
