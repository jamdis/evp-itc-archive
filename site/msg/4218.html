Hi Rita

I only choose the clear plastic so I could see the three component 
devices inside. (The pretty blue tint of the radio LCD while 
sweeping, the flashing red light as the noise generator is playing, 
and the blue hue of the amp microphone while it is on).  The chamber 
is closed enough to allow the sound waves to bounce around and mix.  
Any of the materials you mentioned would work, with some very very 
slight differences in sound, I'm sure, based on the properties of 
absortion, reflection, refraction, etc.  And I think the chamber is 
small enough, with the devices close in proximity enough not impact 
the sound regardless of which material is used.

As for controlling from outside, only with my prototype is there a 
need to open the box/chamber after each session. I redesigned after 
that. The three production units (Prod 1, Prod 2, Prod 3)I built each 
have three access holes, which are rubber grommeted. These allow you 
to leave the chamber closed, and still allow you to start and stop 
the noise generator, up or down sweep the radio, and turn the radio 
on or off. You only open the chamber once, to remove the cable and 
turn on the microphone, then you can keep it closed and control the 
other devices with a small push pole the remainder of the time.  You 
only need to open the chamber again when you are done with all your 
sessions.

- Mike
