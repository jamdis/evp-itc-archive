First it has to have the digital, or number display instead of the 
needle that moves across a dial. Then I have go inside with a scope, 
or meter looking for a point that is changing voltages while a tuning 
button is held down. Then I try to find the source of the tuning 
voltage so I can cut the trace coming from the processor. Every once 
in a while I find a radio that has the radio functions marked, like 
in the Radio Shack 12-469, or 820. The 12-470 has similar markings, 
but it's alot more difficult to use. 

 I use to think I had to use older digital car radios, but I'm find 
newer radio usable, like digital clock radios, and radio/cd players.
