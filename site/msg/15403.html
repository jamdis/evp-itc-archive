> Information for people who attemp to build my circuits, but can't get it to 
> work. The white noise transistor is pretty critical. If it's out put is too 
> low you won't be able to get a signal through the first low pass filter, the 
> one with the 82K resistors. I've been trying to work the bugs out of the 
> systems, and that's one thing that has driven me nuts the last few weeks. I 
> had one board that was working and another, exactly the same that would not 
> work. It turned out to be the transistor, as soon as I put in a Radio Shack 
> 2N3904, the thing started to work. So, it seems you need cheap generic 
> transistors that are noisy for the RVG to work properly. I bought a pack of 
> "NPN swithcing transistors" The 3904's seem to work the best, but the 2222's 
> will work if you select one that has a high white noise output. The 
> transistors that I normally use were form mouser.com, and the output was 
> only about .005v peak peak to peak. With the 2N3904's, the output is about 
> .05 volts. You almost have to have a scope to build these circuits. The 
> scope I use is just a cheappy, I paid $10 for it at a garage sale.
