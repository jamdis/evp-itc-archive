I think that is a great starting point Ann, posting both the raw file
and the altered one. 

Most people do "clean" their files in all forms of EVP but, it is
manipulating it or changing it from it's original, even if that is
just amping it. But, now here's an issue... what you may consider as
simple cleaning, others may not. Others may feel it is okay to do much
more and still feel they have not manipulated the file. "It is what it
is" changes to "It isn't what it was". Some files on here are so
incredibly clear while others sound more like what actually happens. 

I fully understand that the communication received is not always in a
question and answer format. But, here is an example... If I hear and
record "The water is blue" and I asked nothing about the water and the
comment is completely random than is it still considered
communication? Is it conveying any information? More importantly, is
that something that matters? What do we hold as a standard for
communication? I think it's difficult at best to know how to move
forward without having a good idea of where we are now. How can we
improve?

I don't think I'm fretting about station time counting. I think it's a
logical thing we need to consider. It is an easy one to do. Record
your session making note of the time of each word or response. Is it
consistent in time? It should not be. 

I mentioned in a reply to Frank R about a file I'm analyzing. One of
the great things about this file is that I can hear both the
investigators speaking as well as communication from the device. For
me, it is a perfect example of how when we ask a question, our brains
ignore everything until something that makes sense to us is heard. It
is taking me an incredibly long time to transcribe it because of the
need to repeat a section countless times to hear all the layers,
needing to amp it in places, slow it down in others so I know it needs
to be done. However I leave the file raw. On my site,I have posted
some of my traditional EVP's and if I have done anything to them I
include that in the file name; whoareyouamp.wav for example. 

Thank you for you willingness to have an open discussion.

Babs
