> You'll note, while the chip is surface mount, it still has only two sides of pins. In radios that use all digital tuning, the chips usually are four sided, and smaller.
>
> The solder sucker is my preferred method of component removal, some like solder wick instead. I suck out the solder, then re-heat each pin while gently wiggling the module till any remaining solder coools, leaving the pin loose, so I can gently work the module out of the main board. I save the carcas for parts, like capacitors and just incase I have to go back a re-measure a voltage. Then, my grandson takes the old stuff home for his "transporter" he's going to build.
