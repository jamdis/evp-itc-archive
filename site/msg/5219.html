Misty bruce halliday wrote: Ron, I have not heard of any voice recognition systems that would enable you to recognise a recorded evp from a box or traditional. I believe the simple reason being that evp's that come from any type of box we use today run on the same principle, the words are created by the entities using random bits of radio signal or pieces of radio chatter that is being scanned by the box. Traditional evp is based on the theory that the spirit leaving the message uses the ambient energy in the surrounding area and manipulates it into words that are captured by the recorder. both of these methods are very monotone is essence the box replies are made up of different word fragments and not fluid enough. The traditional evp message sounds very whispered or pitchy and is filled with white noise and recorder static. The voice recognition software being used today is like misty said very expensive and is designed to recognise the inflections in human speech it is mainly used by forensic labs to determine if two speech patterns are the same. My opinion is that the software would not be capable of detecting evp messages, I have not personally tried it but had the idea many moons ago and researched the possibility.
