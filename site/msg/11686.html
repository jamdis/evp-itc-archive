I use a digital SLR (Cannon Digital Rebel xti) with a big flash
mounted on it either directed up at the ceiling or with a diffuser
attached to avoid dust orbs. Occasionally I also will set it for a
long exposure to capture some cool architecture or something, but I
won't use any of those photos as evidence just because there are so
many normal things that can happen during a long exposure to make
something odd show up in the pic. 
In most situations with the right lens (I love my Canon 50mm f1.8) you
can adjust the iso and f-stop to take a good picture in low light with
a fast shutter speed without needing the flash. If you don't
understand how all the settings (f-stop, iso speed, shutter speed)
work together I'd suggest getting yourself a basic photography book or
taking a class at your local camera shop. You'd be amazed how many
"ghost pictures" will start just looking like camera operator error to
you after a while. haha
