Hi Everyone,

I've purchased both the Puck and the Ovilus earlier this year and 
while I'm still experimenting with both, I thought I'd weigh in with 
my thoughts.

Concerning Ron's issues, below, re the Ovilus:

1.  I've had no problem with the 9V battery draining quickly when 
using the Ovilus.  I've used it for over 12 hours already and the 
battery still has about 50 percent charge left.

2.  Ron, I noticed that if I pushed the mode selection button down 
too quickly, it does not change mode.  However, I discovered that if 
I pushed and released the button slowly, the mode selection worked 
fine.  Perhaps you're pushing the button too fast?

3.  Dictionary mode only repeated words specific to the location; 
e.g., I had the Ovilus on the battlefields in Gettysburg and it kept 
repeating "crouch, hide, fierce, energy, remember, forgive"; whereas 
on a private investigation at an adult toy store in SF, it kept 
repeating "erotic".  

4.  Both Phoneme and Mix modes worked fine off available energy.  
Again, the locale/environment is key in that both the Ovilus and the 
Puck take readings from the environment (EMF, temperature, etc.) and 
translate those readings into words.

5.  I have not had a chance to do any significant experimentation 
with the other modes (Yes/No, EMG, Level Mode and Dowsing Mode) so 
can't remark on those.

The only issue I have with the Ovilus is that at times it's 
difficult to make out what the robotic voice is saying as it 
distorts a bit on certain words and/or speaks them drawn out 
like "Lis--ten" for Listen.

In regards to the Paranormal Puck, I'm totally in love with it in 
that one can set alarms/thresholds that when reached, will either 
record a video or photo (with your webcam) or audio of whatever's in 
the immediate environment.  Therefore, one doesn't even have to be 
present when the EMF rises to say, over 2 MG, or the temperature 
drops more than 10 degrees.  The webcam will document whatever's in 
the environment at that time.  The Puck's Excel data sheet logs all 
changes in the environmental -- excellent source of reference! 

Sharon from San Francisco
