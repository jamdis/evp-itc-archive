Hi Bill,

I was actually at The Stanley event held earlier this year in late 
March, when Mark Macy was there as a speaker, and where Chris Moon 
first introduced the "Spirit Box" with Chris Coffey.  So I didn't 
have the good fortune of meeting Frank S. as I wasn't at the November 
Stanley event. :(

My first exposure to the Spirit Box at The Stanley caused me to 
wonder why Chris and Chip had to interpret 90 percent of everything 
that was coming through; so in other words, it wasn't very clear 
what, if anything, was being communicated.  It certainly gave us much 
fodder to chew on though!

I tend to view the ITC ghost/spirit boxes as akin to a kind of 
electronic Ouija board -- instead of a planchette-pointer on a board 
with letters in the alphabet to spell out answers, we're supplying 
the spirits with radio frequencies and sound bites to rearrange and 
form into audible words.  With this in mind, one must be cautious in 
conducting experiments in one's home because one never knows what one 
is inviting into one's home.  

Regardless, I'm glad that we're all trying, learning, and sharing.  
Like the early medieval alchemists, many who have risked their own 
lives (or unintentionally grew hair on the soles of their feet) by 
ingesting some of their own concoctions, we're part of a pioneering 
group of spirit chasers that began back in the 19th century.  Because 
of lack of funding from the government in this field, I frankly don't 
know if we'll be able to obtain scientifically-validated proof of our 
ITC experiments in our own lifetime, but I like to think that 500 
years down the road, the people in the future will look back at what 
we're doing today as pioneers, maybe not as charming as the early 
alchemists are portrayed in Durer woodcuts, but pioneers nonetheless.

Sharon
