To expand upon Guy's post, the echo chamber size can vary. Some of his
boxes have an internal chamber that's about 2" x 3.5". Some of his
boxes have chambers which are external and are a bit larger. An
external one I saw was made with a light wood frame which was wrapped
in heavy aluminum foil.

If someone is considering constructing their own FB then I suggest
considering the sweep tuning method also as the recordings I've heard
are impressive. We are experimenting with a RVG equipped box and are
getting good results but I am also interested in the sweep tuning
approach.

> 
> The key component that can be difficult to find is the Sanyo LA1135 
> AM Tuner IC that's at the heart of the system. Frank has apparently 
> scrounged this chip from old car radios.
> 
> Here's how it operates:
> 
> The RVG (Random Voltage Generator) output is fed to the "tuning" 
> input of the LA1135 AM radio receiver. What this basically does is 
> similar to a human being rapidly and randomly hitting the tuning 
> station buttons in a car radio or varying the tuning dial of a 
> stationary or portable radio. The output of this randomly-tuned AM 
> radio is then fed to a small speaker into an internal "echo chamber" 
> (although his box also provides an output jack for an external 
> speaker to use an external "echo chamber" or even just the room) 
> which also contains a small electret microphone (these are the types 
> that a very tiny and can be clipped to your lapel, tie, etc.). The 
> output of this mike is then fed to another speaker (or optionally to 
> headphones) for the output. Where the "entities" interact with the 
> box is in this "echo chamber" (probably just a small, soundproof 
> plastic box) using the speech snippets from the randomly-tuned AM 
> radio stations as a carrier for any EVP.
