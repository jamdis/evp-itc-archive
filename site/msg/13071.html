Thanks for the clarification Mike. I would still warn that, even in
your experiment here, you yourself could become your own weak link in
objectivity. As Cheri pointed out, even someone with the best
intentions, who truly wants to provide accurate & honest information,
is only human and subject to error. This is especially true if they
have already decided which parts of their theories are right or wrong,
without, again, objective testing. This is not a phenomenon that only
effects the home experimenter, but experts in all fields, and they
have learned of methods over time (such as the double blind testing I
mentioned) to help weed out weak processes and develop and explore
their theories on the firmest foundations possible.

But I understand you are not trying to prove or test anything in this
case, or use them to claim any kind of proof: you're just trying an
experiment. So, good luck with it.
