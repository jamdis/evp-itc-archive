Well, "the box" in nothing more than a radio with swept tuning--as far as I
know and have experienced, sweep across the radios tuning range, or band
generates bits of speech music and noise that the entities use to reform
into their voices, although there maybe more involved, but I don't know how
one would test it.

 Most of the boxes for sale out there are the hack radio boxes. Many of the
modern digital radios have a function that mutes, or quiets the radio while
tuning between stations. I'm not sure why, but unmutting the radio allows
the audio to be heard while the radio searches for the next
station--and--the radio is disallowed form locking onto a station, so it
keeps sweeping all the way to the end of the band--AM or FM, then starts
over. This creates the raw audio and "they" speak. The sweep can even be
done by hand, tuning a radio slowing by hand. I sue cheap radios I get at
thrift stores for this, gluing a dowel to the tuning knob to make it easier.
Then record form the speaker--open air.

 So as long as the radio sweeps at not to fast and not too slow, it'll
works. Some are better than others.

 It takes some practice, and maybe even a certain allowing to hear the ghost
voices.

 I prefer to take a tuner out of a radio, such as a clock radio and supply
an external sweep voltage. The sweep circuits are just basic op amps, and
you can buy a function generator kit to supply a triangle wave for linear
sweep. See keyportparanormal.com for more info on using this kit, and
building boxes.

 One problem with this stuff is that it can be extremely subjective, others
may not hear what you hear, or hear it the same. I think mostly the messages
are meant for the one that receives it. We go round and round with the
skeptics and debunkers.
