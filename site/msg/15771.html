In a message dated 8/19/2009 9:08:27 A.M. Eastern Daylight Time, warrior18eii@... writes: Assumptions and expectations!Assumptions and expectations!? I hear a lot of both of these concepts in your words and when mentioning your research. Cognitive functioning or processing sensory information in order to come to a conclusion or judgement is how we function in this world. In order for your mind to understand this physical world you live in it automatically makes certain assumptions about sensory information it takes in. Your eyes expect to see objects oriented correctly, with the correct color, lighting, reflectivity and shading, and you expect there size and shape to be within scale compared to other objects. My point is your senses have a range, which implies limitations. Since this is correct for your senses it is also correct for your ability to understand perspectives with any wide ranging diversity. You are able to see and process the information of our physical world just as any other human being does. That is great for a human but why would you think for a second, you co of a mind and voice that speaks through a radio from some unknown location. The information you receive from the other side of your radios or even from thin air through EVP's could be completely correct and true no matter what the context because you don't know what perspective it is coming from. You are making a lot of unfounded assumptions about the (dare I say) beings or worlds they exist in and have a lot of expectations about the information you receive. You are looking for answers to the wrong questions! Quit assuming that these entities know you or the people around you. Quit assuming they are here for your entertainment as a personal fortune teller. Quit expecting them to know answers to question they may have themselves (just because they aren't physical doesn't mean they know everything). Don't assume you will be speaking to a dead relative or someone you once knew. Go to a fortune teller if you want information about the furture. If you are serious about researching these voices and the equipment used to record them then ask the right questions. If you have a box made by Frank then ask Frank how the different circuits work, what is the puropose of some of the options, why is the RVG better or worse than a circuit without it? You have a unique situation here where the inventor of an important piece of equipment is willing to share the most minute details of how it works without a price tag attached. Some of the other box variations are sold by one person but created by someone else and usually based on some loose design similar to Frank's. The other equipment made by Bill Chapp and others will probably stay a mystery because they are sold for profit. Most people who use the different "hacks" know how to make them but don't know much about electronic theory or the behavior of radio waves to understand how they work. Point is you have plenty of questions to ask the human's who have built the equipment you are using before you can even begin to ask questions of the entities you are communicating with.
