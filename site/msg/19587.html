I also use SpecterCam (SC)on Fact or Faked and off the show. The SC3 is certainly the most advanced model and I use that one on the series since at 600 lines, it's the highest resolution and we shoot in hi-def. It's priced at $340 but you have the added benefit of an adjustable image intensifier (think military night vision goggles) plus autoswitching power supply between 24VAC or 12VDC for long distance cord runs or battery power. It has many other features.

The SC2 is a good compromise between price/performance with 500 lines at $230 plus it has unique features like motion detection and with 12VDC it can have resonable length power cords or battery power. It is slightly more compact than the SC1 or SC3.

The SC1 is the way for people who want to experiement with full spec cams to get into this research and with 380 lines at $130 its very affordable. It runs on 24VAC so the cord runs can be long enough to go room to room if power isn't available where you want the camera set up.

All have good sized and sensitive CCD's coupled with a good lens and have 75ohm video out BNC that you plug into a recorder or DVR. In darkened locations they are fine right out of the box but if the location is brightly lit, like it was in the Rolling Hills conference room, I attached a douple pass filter that allows UV and IR to pass but inhibits much of the visible spectrum. I did this to avoid overexposing the image. I offered the SC1 at $120 at the conference and am glad to extend that deal to anyone here in the group.

I have been offering the SC cams at all conferences I attend after purchasing them for use on the series. I really like them and compared them to other full spectrum solutions first. BTW, a more accurate term for virtually all cams that see into invisible spectrums is wide spectrum since the lens absorb some of the wavelength but the term full spectrum is what people have come to know. All of the SC models are capable of capturing very good video and light anomalies are showing up in the invisible spectrums which is encouraging this research.

There are more features of each camera than what I listed here but wanted to list some of the highlights of each to help clarify the differences between models. 

A new one I now have is the SpecterCamCorder and as the name implies, it has on board media storage via SD cards (not included) plus an LCD touchscreen viewfinder. As is the case with all smaller cameras, there are limits and advantages to downsizing but the compromise is in the ability to gather light onto the imager. It goes for $284 and is a well done mobile camera that is the best of it's type in my opinion.
