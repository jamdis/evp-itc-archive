Oh, this is so interesting! Email is such a difficult medium through which to communicate, never mind EVP or ITC!! (No pun intended on the "medium" part... oh, ok, if it works, let it go!) I find David's posts interesting and helpful in terms of things to think about regarding what "validation" might be useful in analyzing the "how" of these phenomena. Ok, so I don't always like what I interpret as his tone of voice, but that's the difficulty about email... we can't hear his tone of voice, nor do we have any sense of how he is speaking when he writes his emails. I know in my business consulting, I have to be meticulous to NOT write anything that can be misinterpreted due to lack of tone of voice. People write things that they think are not inflammatory, and others read them as totally hostile... we all know this happens! Personally, I have experienced coherent voices coming through a simple recorder (a cassette tape at that, years ago), but I have not been experimenting myself lately, though I would like to make the time to do so. I have concerns about the stray signals that a device that sweeps the am and/or fm and/or cb bands might generate, and so if I were doing my experiments now, I'd likely NOT use a box or a hacked radio. But: that does not mean I intend to invalidate anyone else's work. I love knowing what others here are reporting, and listening to the occasional voice file (I get the digest so I don't get the files attached). I am delighted when "messages" come through. The sheer number of coherent hits with this suggests to me that at least some of these phenomena are genuine, if not many of them. I for one appreciate David's thinking and analyses, and am inclined to think similarly to him, and I for one hope he continues to contribute here, though I do suggest to you, David, that you maybe try to moderate your email tone of voice . The more opinions and conversations we have here on this list about ALL the possibilities in this fascinating area of research, the better... and that's my two cents!
