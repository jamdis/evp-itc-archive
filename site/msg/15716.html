I'm completely new to this, but I've had some really good success, so I'll answer a couple of your questions per my own experiences so far -- see below:


> What I do want to ask, and maybe someone could explain for me, what *is* the benefit of asking the Box a question.
 
For me, I have an agenda:  I want to speak to my dead father and brother.  Since I haven't had success with that so far, I'm speaking to anyone I get, which so far claims to be a guy named Steve, a guy named Frank, someone named Chris, two dead friends of my father's whom I didn't know where dead (Gordy and Greg), my grandmother (Margaret), and possibly two people named Ed.  I ask questions of these entities in an effort to find out what's become of my father and brother, who both died suddenly, and just generally to try to prove life after death.

> Also - and please don't anyone yell at me, when I see the video's, > I see people on occasion grasping at what they think they might hear.
 
When I started, I expected crisp, clean words, or I wouldn't accept them as words; I wasn't going to bend noise into what I wanted to hear.  Then I started to realize that "they" couldn't make the right words crisp and clear every time; I realized this because I was hearing patterns, the same or nearly the same things over and over, but each set slightly different.  So yes, I do accept slightly garbled versions of words, and don't expect to hear a perfect sentence -- but usually the answer is repeated two or three times in a row.  For instance, I recorded them saying different versions of "I love you," -- all with the clear word love -- twelve different times in a row.  Even though only a few were crisp, clear "I love you's" or "love you's", you could tell that all twelve were trying to say the same thing.

I do not clean anything.  I tape onto regular, old-fashioned tape with a four-track machine meant for music.
 
> Do you use it to prove or disprove spirit in a particular location?

I use it on my dining room table.

> Does one hear more 'male' or 'female' sounding voices, can on determine the gender of the voice?
