This is fascinating.  I have every work that Tesla published in my 
library, including a collection of all his patents.  I've studied all of 
it thoroughly. I knew that there was a collection of unpublished works 
which the US government confiscated after he died.  A certain group was 
offered this entire collection of unpublished works back in the 50's but 
declined to take them.  So I wondered what happened to them - if they had 
been destroyed or if they were stored.

I'd like to get my hands on some of that material!

Tesla once worked for Edison but there was personal friction between the 
two and they parted ways. It was after that that Tesla developed 
alternating-current systems and Edison still had his money invested in 
direct-current power distribution.  When Tesla's AC systems gained favor, 
it did not make Edison happy.

Mark Twain became a very close friend of Tesla.  I have some photos taken 
in Tesla's New York lab where Clemens (Twain) is standing with a ball of 
lightning between his hands.

It wouldn't surprise me that Tesla had received some EVP. His equipment 
was rather crude by today's standards and not very sensitive but I could 
envision it happening.
