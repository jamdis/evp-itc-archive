Be a bit careful of formats like = mp3 if you are doing a detailed analysis of the = sounds though.   While some formats = (like wav) can store the audio very close to= the way it was recorded, formats like mp3= compress the file, and in so doing lose s= ome of the sonic details.  This may n= ot be a big deal in music, where the audio= is fairly clear to begin with.  But = with sound sources with a lot of noise (li= ke evps), this can not only obscure any re= al sounds, but pixelize non-sounds (like white = noise) and make them start to sound like = speech.

So mp3 might be good for sharing = the data, once you've already formed an opinion = based on an analysis of the wav file.  But = mp3 is not good as a medium for analysis.  = It can lead to many false positives and = misinterpretations.
