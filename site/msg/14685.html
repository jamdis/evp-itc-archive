Silver is the best conductor known, with a conductivity of 63.01 E06 Siemens
per meter. Copper is next best at 59.6 E06 S/m. Gold is not the best, at
45.2 E06 S/m. Aluminum is much farther down, at 37.8 E06 S/m.

Silver and gold are expensive. They are usually used to plate RF assemblies
where the skin effect takes place (current doesn't flow uniformly through a
conductor at RF frequencies but mainly travels at the surface of the
conductor). Gold has the advantage that it never tarnishes or corrodes with
most things it is exposed to. It's used a lot in contacts for rotary
switches and relays.
