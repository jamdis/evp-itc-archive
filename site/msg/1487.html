Thanks. I use ExpressPCB, they are fairly low cost. The next boards will be 
pretty complex, with lots of through holes, and at least two layer, and I 
may have to use a mix of SMT, and standard components so I can use both 
sides to get the board size down, sq. inches=$$. The layout I was working on 
last winter was close to $500 for 10 boards, and that was before adding 
linear sweep, the tone generator, and it was an AM only tuner. Perf board is 
much cheaper, but it's time consumming to build one at a time. 
http://www.expresspcb.com/

I had one set of three boards made that were all SMT last year, but they 
didn't include the tuner. They were ExpressPCB's "mini boards", at $60 for 
the three boards.  Before that I had two proto type boards made that cost 
$162, of course I gave all those away! Yeah, my wife went nuts long ago cuz 
of my non-comprehension of money.

I'm dragging my feet getting the boards made because I've run into a curious 
problem with this stuff, the circuit conditions seem to change. With some 
boards I have  DC offeset problem that I have to use pull down reisistors on 
every op amp stage to compensate for. I just made a board, and had to remove 
all the pull downs, or it wouldn't work at all, the offset problem missing, 
and I don't know what changed, as I'm getting the same parts from the same 
distributor. The boards in various systems seem to stop working sometimes, 
and I think it has something to do with this problem. I was thinking about 
adding a dip swtich, and pull downs, so they can be added when, or if 
needed. If the DC offset is too large in the random sweep section, it swamps 
out the signal, so nothing comes through. I prefer analog, but perhaps a 
digital solution is called for, as in a an RNG to generate the random pulses 
I derive from white noise, and that's another reason why I'm not jumping at 
the PCB's yet, I don't have a final design------yet.

Frank-S
