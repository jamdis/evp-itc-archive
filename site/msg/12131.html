I was highly skeptical, and highly critical of box-type communication.
I began looking all over the internet, youtube, etc... wondering why
people put so much stock into these specialized tuners or modified
radios. It then occurred to me that I was being skeptical of something
that I have never sat down and tried. Not having much money, I went
with the shack hack method. My first session was phenomenal, and
although I don't know how mystery voices can accurately and
contextually answer my questions posed to what is, in effect, a
malfunctioning radio, I am only left with the knowledge that it works.
At least it worked for me. Believe me, it drives me insane trying to
figure out the mechanism by which spirits would be able to communicate
with a box spitting out chunks of broadcast fragments. That being
said, though, it really isn't anymore of a leap of faith than the
coil-induction theory of evp collection, I mean, how would a spirit
know how to manipulate focused emf fields to produce corresponding
frequency. 

As to what the voices might be, I have to say as far as I am
concerned, the jury is still out. I am wondering if there isn't some
sort of latent PK element in play by the operator, it would certainly
explain why the information is so accurate and specific. I am working
on a "blind" test to study that to my own satisfaction. The phenomenon
may very well be external entities trying to make contact, and if so,
that is just as cool. Either way, having thought the concept was
absurd at one time, I simply gave it a shot and the results were
undeniable. Honestly, subsequent attempts at ITC with the shack hack
have produced mixed results, none with the in-your-face accuracy of
the first time. Suffice it to say it is dissapointing I cannot have
that kind of success every time, but I can certainly say that there is
 a force at work here and my curiosity is understandibly aroused.

thanks
