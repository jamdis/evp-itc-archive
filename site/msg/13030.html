Interesting your comment about the sentences, and you are correct they do occur.
 
With my process each question is one .wav file, but I am finding that I still at times need to break up a .wav file into three or more files, just so I can hear the messages more clearly. it appears at times the messages are compressed as in a zip file into one .wav file.
 
I am very happy that you are using EVP to help find the missing.
 
Each of us in this field of study have two barriers that hinder the success of our work.
1. getting law enforcement to listen to us and take us seriously
2. keeping other spirits in our recording space quiet whil we talk to our victim. When they have side conversations, their messages get picked up thus corrupting the data.
 
For number 1, I think we and perhaps even some mediums having the same issue, keep trying to get law enforcement to look at our leads, but I think we need to think backwards. Meaning, lets get law enforcement to start using the device themselves, and once they see the value in it, then they may be more willing to accept our evp as leads letting us help them.
 
For those of us, like you, me and others that volunteer our time recoridng EVP to help find the missing, we should try and find a willing police detective to do a pilot with us in that we record some of their cold case files, and they agree to try and follow up on the leads.
 
Right now I am actually working with an investigator on a child case, and this investigator believes in what we do, is very knowlegeable of EVP, but has not told her peers where the leads are coming from.  We need to get past this fear.
 
Thanks for sharing.
