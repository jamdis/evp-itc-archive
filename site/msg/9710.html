> itcdeadpeople <ITCdeadpeople@...> wrote:          Hi Kay. Just use the patch cord I 
described in my previous response ... connect it from the 
> headphone jack on the recorder to the mic jack on your computer. Open Audacity (or 
> whatever), hit the record button, then hit the play button on the recorder to play the 
> session into the computer. 
> 
> When you're done, press the stop button on the recording software, the stop button on 
> the recorder itself, and then save your session. From there, you can begin to work with 
it, 
> without worry of losing any data. Just be sure when you close the file DON'T SAVE 
> CHANGES on the original, so you always have the raw version to refer back to.
> 
> You can do this same thing with any recorder that has an earphone jack. Just go with the 
> one you are drawn to. There are a bunch of them on eBay. If you are doing sweep, you 
> don't even need a digital recorder, just plug in a mic into your computer and record on 
it. 
> This saves you all the time and hassle of moving the file from the recorder into your 
> computer. That's what I do. You really only need a digital recorder for traditional work 
or 
> portability (unless you have a laptop, which is pretty easy to take with you.) :-)
