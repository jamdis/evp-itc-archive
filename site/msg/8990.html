Hi Jeremy.  If everyone knew about these support boards and that other resources are 
available to them, that would be one thing.  Obviously everyone here is aware of that.  But 
not everyone knows this place exists.  

And even if they do know about the boards here, how many would have the nerve to ask 
for help from strangers?  As Bruce recently shared, he gives people invitations to contact 
him privately in case they are too shy just ask a question publicly.  Which means, by the 
way, to some degree they have overcome some of that shyness in order to post whatever it 
was to begin with, which inspired him to make this offer.  What if they don't even have the 
gumption to be anything more than be a look-i-loo?

I'm guessing many people are just surfing the net, checking out paranormal stuff, and run 
across the hacked boxes for sale.  

Again, I ask people who are taking the position you and Bruce are regarding profits ... do 
you have this same harsh sentiment toward Ron Ricketts, who makes and sells the 
MiniBoxes?  I realize he is passionate about this research and helping others.  Does that 
give him amnesty for making a profit?  

Personally I think we need to not be so judgmental.  At what point does "profitable gain in 
the paranormal field" become "sickening?"  I have a friend who is a medium.  I mean THE 
REAL DEAL.  He charges for his services.   The fee is reasonable compared to many in his 
field.  Does he do this because he's a terrible person looking to take advantage of people?  
NO, he does so because he has been given a gift that changes people's lives for the better.  
But he has to make a living... has to support himself in some way.  And because he has 
chosen to provide this service full time (and is not a trust fund baby), he makes a profit.  
This allows him to help far more people than he could if working some other kind of job 
and providing his services at no cost on the side.  

I realize this is not exactly the same thing as gouging people on the price for a 
commercially available product, but making such blanket statements about profitability in 
the paranormal field being sickening is rather short-sighted, in my opinion.  

I have a southern friend who likes to say, "It's a mighty thin plank that ain't got two sides."  
I just don't think we have the right to claim that simply because someone is offering a 
good and or service for profit to consumers in the paranormal market, that this 
automatically means they are taking advantage of other's vulnerabilities.  

For Pete's sake, no one is twisting the people's arms to buy those units on eBay.  They are 
perfectly free to buy from whatever source they choose.  Since the units do work as 
claimed, we can argue that people have overpaid, shopped unwisely, or whatever.  I assure 
you this happens every single day throughout the retail market place with all types of 
products and services.    

I will say I have a fairly heathy dose of distain for charlatans/scam artists (of any flavor in 
any field) but that is not what we are talking about here.  We are talking about people who 
are offering legitimate products to consumers.  I'd rather see people doing this for profit, 
even when obscene, than running scams that damage people's physical, mental, and/or 
financial well-being.  

Margaret
