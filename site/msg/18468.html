This is a box I made back in March with single supply(one set of batteries)
linear and random sweep. Most of the time random hangs up and never starts.
The cure isn't real elegant, but it works. I use a variable voltage
regulator to supply the DC offset for the random pulse amplifier, and use a
panel mount trim pot to tweak it as needed. I have a resisitor attached to
both ends of the pot to limit it's range.  The DC offset is needed in the
single, or dual supply versions, the dual supply doesn't hang up though.

 I asked how they like random, and hillbilly woman responds "hear'd ya--it's
lousy" at two seconds. This is one of thing about random, it seems to get
more real, or natural sounding voices. At 9 seconds "it's perfect--I need
it"(?).

 Wihtout the panel mounted trim, it will work, but it takes up to 20
minutes, I can cut that down maybe five minute by shifting the DC level in
increments. Again with dual supply, 30 seconds and the random mode is up and
running.

 So maybe moving the box forward means going back to waht i started with?
