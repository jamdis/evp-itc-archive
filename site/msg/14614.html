What you are describing is known in audio engineering as 
 beam-directed ultrasonic demodulation. The principle has been well
 described over the years and was in fact mentioned at a recent
 conference in Finland in 2008. The technology is most commonly 
 applied to parametric loudspeaker arrays typical of Audio Spotlight.

 Audio signals are modulated selectively onto an ultrasonic carrier 
 which tends to be in the 10 to 20 KHz region. This is explained by  
 simple physics and seems to have to do with the way in which bone  
 conductance occurs in humans.

 So it is possible for humans to hear in the range well above 20
 KHz. Studies of brains of animals well-known for their ultrasonic
 abilities confirm the neurotopology involved.

 Now as to the possibility that this same thinking can be applied to
 some form of vibrational transformation. As I say often "anything
 is possible, it's just a matter of probability". So how probable is
 what is proposed? It seems that such things could indeed happen.
 But is this necessary? 

 Many people including myself have suggested for many years that a
 probable source of disembodied voices may be psychokinesis. 
 In other words the phenomena is actually self-produced and often  
 self-directed. 

 Just a little while ago I got off the phone with a colleague on the
 West coast who is well known in the parapsychology community. He
 was telling me of a case over 20 years ago where a man's voice was
 heard on an audio recording made when the man and my colleague were
 standing not a few feet apart. Even though the man was not
 physically speaking, his speech was clearly heard on the tape. 
 Even the speech style was clearly audible and consistent with his
 penchant for swearing. This was not in the noise; this would be
 classified as a Class A EVP. And it was not heard at the time of
 the recording, so it is an EVP as defined by this group.

 Given this, I think the more probable explanation is that what is
 occurring at least some of the time is PK in fact and likely not
 discarnate communications. I am not saying it never happens, just
 positing an interesting alternative explanation which has a basis
 in factual observation of very high quality in a site that has been
 well-documented even on recent television programs in Los Angeles
 as having paranormal disturbances which go back many decades.
