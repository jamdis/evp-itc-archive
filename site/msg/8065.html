Misty juli velazquez wrote: William, I know this was directed for Misty, but if I may I would like to also answer your question. The natural back ground noise is used as a "carrier" to reveal the presence of undetectable signals in an ambient. Misty and many others use water as a source. I use tap water running in an ambient as the source of white noise. Other noise sources can be used, such as a fan, or AM radio tuned between the stations (which is also used by many on this board.) It is important to call your attention to the act that the voices are very weak, and trained ears are necessary for their identification in most cases. Some researchers have upgraded their systems using a germanium diode plugged into the tape recorder's microphone input (1N34 or equivalent). The noise generated by this component, and also ambient detected radio signals, are used as well. I have in the past also left a fan running as wind source, which also proved effective. I applaud those who focus on this form of communication, for its a lot of hard work and in my opinion.
