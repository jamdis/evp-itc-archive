> My understanding of this device is that it has a white noise generator that
> you adjust when your recorder is in record mode while the speaker output is
> plugged into the device.  The idea is to adjust the white noise so that
> there is no spike on the meter.  This ideally would set the white noise
> level as being the same as the room in which you are in and fine tuned to
> the inherent noise in your recorder.  Once this is done, any noise that goes
> above this white noise threshold would cause a jump in the LED meter.
>  Obviously, during the questioning part of an EVP session, your voice would
> register on the LED's as being above the white noise threshold.  The idea is
> to look at the meters when no one is talking to see if you get an EVP.   The
> EVP should also trigger the meters if it is above the noise level of the
> room.  I looked at buying one of these back in May when they were first made
> available, but I didn't think it would be worth the $145 the group was
> charging for them because any cough, rustle of jackets, or movement would
> also trigger the LED's and in a group with more than one person in a
> session, this could create frustrating false positives.
