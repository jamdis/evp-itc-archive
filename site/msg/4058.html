Hi LC,

The two setups I did were:

1) Three 12-469's through a mixer, mixed with white noise (performed 
with Audacity), my voice via a microphone, and all that audio 
captured using a free program called "Scanner Recorder". You can 
download it here: http://www.davee.com/scanrec/
I have not really taken that unit anywhere to test it properly.  I 
only did the original testing you hear on the video.

2) One 12-470 placed in a "chamber", with a noise generator (used 
a "My Lil' Reminder" 20 second digital recorder/player) with recorded 
white/pink noise I captured while in Florida.  Also inside is 
a "Listen Up" amplified microphone and an Olympus digital recorder.
The only wiring is a Y-connector which connects the output from the 
amplified mic to the input of the recorder, and also to ear-buds for 
listening to what is being recorded. You can also plug a small 
speaker into this if desired.
The raw audio from the linear sweep of either the AM or FM band is 
projected from the 12-470 speaker, into the closed "chamber", along 
with the audio from the speaker of the noise generator ("My Lil' 
Reminder") unit.  Those two sources, plus any ambient noise inside 
the "chamber" is picked up by the amplified mic and fed into the 
recorder and ear-buds. The recorder produces the .wav file.
You can use a 12-469 instead of the 12-470. You just need to connect 
a small speker to the output of the 12-469.

As with my three 12-469 system, I have so far only done the initial 
test run on th 12-470 system.  I hope to bring both units to a place 
where I can do some better experimentation.  I have 2 weeks off near 
Christmas time, so that is probably when I'll be doing that.

I hope I have answered your questions, LC.
