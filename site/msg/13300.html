Something I forgot to clarify in my last email.... ITC is not a belief system, belief is not a requisite for this research. The question of mental competency can only be answered after evaluation by a qualified psychologist. If a person is neglectful or abusive then a child could be removed. Social workers usually work under the license of a state certified psychiatrist (who is an MD) and has to give the final diagnosis of the subject. The social worker can provide therapy, recommendations and such but cannot certify a person incompetent.
 
Its not as easy as it looks to take a child away from thier mother and father. Technically, everything is supposed to be fair when it comes to this subject but in a lot of cases it is not. That depends on how well connected the social services person is and how good of a lawyer you have. Ultimately, even the psychiatrist has to present evidence and recommendations to a judge who makes it official.
 
To answer the question whether a child would be removed from parents who research ITC; highly unlikely!

Your practice of ITC research does not mean you cannot care for your child. Social services do not take your children for your beliefs, they take them because you are not able to care for them properly and/or are abusive. Look at any number of fanatical religious practices or belief systems just in the US and you see a bunch of children being subjected to some pretty scary ideas. Belief is not a requisite for this research, only the desire to communicate.

The condition of 'normal' would be defined as the average of social expectations, which can be completely different from one culture to the next. I think Misty makes some good points, and I agree that the issue is similar to how one exposes a child to a belief system, such as a religion.  I agree, too, that it will depend on the individual situation, as some will do this in a healthy way, and some may not: for example, we could all probably name some religious cults that have been more harmful than good.  In the ITC world, I suppose a bad situation would be one where a poor ITC researcher (one who thinks they are getting results, but isn't) insists to the child that they can hear spirits.
