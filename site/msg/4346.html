> Group;
>      A short time ago, I got the idea that I could do my own EVPs 
> here at home,see my posting, "Newbie" on December 9th. I talked with 
> Mike Coletta recently and he suggested I post how I set up my laptop 
> and also my results.
>      My goal is to turn my laptop into a self-contained, portable EVP 
> recorder.  Here's what I did, and also what happened.  I've posted 
> pictures of the setup under LapTop and Laptopmp3s so you can see and 
> hear what I have been getting.  
>      For software, I am using Audacity, EVPmaker and Audition 1.0. 
> The laptop is a Dell M1210 with a 1.66 ghz duo processor and 1gb of 
> ram.  It is about a year and a half old.  It is a small laptop with 
> little teenie speakers.  For initial playback, I have an enclosed ear 
> type headset.  For portability, I also have earbuds.  Lastly, I went 
> to Fry's (electronic superstore) and bought the smallest, cheapest 
> ($9.95) microphone.  
> Here is how I use all of the above
>  
> First, I make sure the computer knows the microphone is plugged in 
> and that it is working, open the Audition software, click "new", then 
> select "11025, mono, 16bit" from the next menu.
>  
> Second, I start the EVPmaker software and open the 1 minute .wav file 
> I found online of a doctor giving a talk.  I then click the "extras" 
> tab and select "normalize signal."  This basically increases the 
> volume of the EVPmaker output.  "Segments" are set at "80", "Overlap" 
> and "Z-Cross" are checked.  Under "Play EVP", select "continuously".  
> Then I click the yellow play button.  It is now babbling..
> 
> Third, open Audacity software, and under "generate" select "white 
> noise" and create a 10 second clip of white noise.  By 
> holding "shift" on the keyboard while clicking on the play button, it 
> will play the noise as an endless loop.  It usually drowns out the 
> EVPmaker, and the volume is adjusted by using the slider that appears 
> next to the graphic waveform of the opened file, not the volume 
> control that is located higher, under the Play/Pause buttons.  The 
> upper slider adjusts the master volume for .wav files playback and 
> should be left at maximum.  I adjust it so it accompanies the 
> EVPmaker sounds, kind of a background noise and not too loud.  Listen 
> to my files and you can get an idea on how the level of the white 
> noise/EVPmaker is set. 
> 
> Fourth, microphone is placed on the laptop keyboard right next to the 
> touchpad, aimed at the computers speakers.  I use a bit of plumbers 
> putty to hold it in place.  All of the programs can be sized to fit 
> on the screen at the same time.  See picture in photo section.  Next, 
> recording is started in Audition.   I did a few trial runs to get all 
> the sound levels right.  To ask questions, I simply lean forward and 
> speak into the microphone while all programs are running to check the 
> sound levels.  That's it.  I record 3 minutes at a time and ask 3-4 
> questions during that time.  I've noticed the sound from the laptop 
> speakers does bounce off my chest and then the laptop screen, 
> creating a Doppler effect when I lean forward.
>   
> Once everything was working, I started my sessions.  First, I read 
> a "statement/prayer" to clear the air and set forth the rules.  I 
> also remind "them" to be really clear because I willl not do signal 
> processing to pull their messages out .  I get all the software ready 
> to go, then start recording first, so I don't miss anything.  Then I 
> hit play on EVPmaker and Audacity.  
> 
>      My first sessions were done using my desktop computer, and 
> asking questions to see if this setup would work, then I used the 
> same setup the laptop and asked the same questions.  These files are 
> in "setup" folder.  
>      My next sessions were focused on asking about an unusual light 
> that my wife and I saw in the middle of the night in the desert in 
> 1973.  I have no idea what we saw, but now have some answers.  These 
> files are in "desert experience" file.  My questions were not leading 
> in any way, nor did I speak aloud any details of the lights we saw.
>      I have a few other mysterious things that have happened in my 
> life I want to ask about using this system.  I feel more like an 
> experimenter than a researcher.
> 
>     I am absolutely amazed at the answers and statements I 
> have "received" right off the bat.  They are clear  I don't process 
> the recording at all, other than to normalize the files - and 
> relevant to the questions posed.  They range from Class A to Class 
> C.  There are some other things I want to try in the future, but for 
> now, I'm happy with the results.
