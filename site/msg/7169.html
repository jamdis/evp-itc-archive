Years (OK, decades) ago when I got my degree in linguistics, I took some graduate coursework in how the brain perceives spoken language. There is a degree to which a sound can waiver from the actual target but still have the brain recognize it. The recordings we get remind me a lot of experiments I did in college where we would program a computer to pronounce a word with a sound somewhere between, for example, "b" and "p", combine it with some more sounds to make a short word, like "bat" or "pat", and see which way the subject perceived it. If the spirits have trouble getting the message through clearly on their end, looking at ways to interpret it better on our end might help.
