Hi Stefan.  Thank you for sharing your observations.  This would be in line with the 
experimental indications of traditional EVP being formed inside the unit, as evidenced by 
digital recorders which have had their internal mic removed, yet can still record voice.  

I started thinking about acoustics when I jumped into the world of radio sweep.  I noted a 
big difference between sweep and traditional EVP, in that with traditional EVP methods, it 
is quite rare for the same EVP to be heard at the same time when multiple recorders are 
running simultaneously.  However, with sweep, I have noticed that multiple units will 
indeed pick up the same messages, and in fact, when I have blended the tracks from the 
two separate units, the message at times was even clearer.  

Perhaps in the case of sweep and the like, the manipulation is actually taking place inside 
the radio, just as appears to be the case with various recorders in traditional EVP?  Since 
the same thing is heard on multiple units with sweep/EVPM, this leads me to believe that 
the change is taking place somewhere along the line prior to hitting the airwaves.  

This can all be so mind boggling!  ;0)

Margaret
