It was claimed by the maker of the RT-EVP that it gets pure EVP because
while it's still a swept radio, it sweeps in an area of the RF
spectrum where there are no radios stations, specifically he said the FM
band in the RT ends at 88.5mhz, where broadcast FM starts--in this country.
That much is not correct, here in Denver we have a sports station at
87.7mhz, and PBS at 88.1mhz, and it is not just this one radio, I can set
these frequencies on any radio and get the stations.  Who knows what's else
is in the supposedly unoccupied areas of any band? Just as in the past, AM
used to go to 1600 khz, it's now at 1710khz. Couple that with the fact that
radio is not absolute, you don't have a station exactly on any given
frequency, and that's the only frequecny it can be received on. All
broadcasts have a certain bandwidth, in AM it's limited to 10khz, I don't
remember what the band width is for FM broadcast, a hundred khz maybe?
Then--too make it worse you can get all kinds of cross modulation effects in
the atmosphere and get signals all over the spectrum.  Then, couple these
inherent problem with a tiny radio, where the circuits can not be made real
tight, unless you want $10,000 radio, there is all kinds of room for
unexpected signals to creep in. That's not even to mention harmonics, all
amplifiers and transmitters produce harmonics that cover a huge spectrum.
You can filter to cut down the harmonics, but it's not 100%.  FCC rules just
say they have to be so many DB down from the main transmission, I don't know
exactly how many DB (decibels), translation, there is still some energy
broadcast across the RF spectrum from any transmitter. Like I said,
atmospherics, what about atmospheric ducting, where VHF signals can be
transmitted great distances in sort of a wave guide formed in the atmosphere
during certain conditions. FM is VHF, very high frequency.  Even at my house
there is still a bunch of garbage at 88.5mhz, where FM is supposed to
start, where the RT's sweep is supposed to end. I probably don't know the
half of it, I'm a ham operator, not an engineer.

 To make matters worse, there are sources of natural radio that cover the
whole RF spectum--I think--I may be wrong about that. You can even get these
natural radio signals from space, from Jupiter and Saturn--the sun. Some
sound like voices, whispers, whistles and moans.

 Sooooo, "pure EVP", No such thing! I don't  like to get all skeptical, but
I'm alot nicer than then the real skeptics out there. It's not my intention
to attack.  If one is making claims, they better know what they are talking
about.

 That said, I think the RT-EVP has merit, and is probably useful, as long as
one understands the limitations. You still have to judge what is received by
content and meaning, and you have to be aware that those little whispers can
be just about anything. White noise EVP is still a valid concept. Just like
the box, some of what is heard is just a broadcast fragments, if it talks
about tires, sports, or hamburgers, it's probably just radio.

 My apologies to the makers of the RT-EVP, the concept is good, I like the
delay feature where you can hear what is received, and the size, you can't
beat the size, I was not privy to it's use, so my experience with
it limited. Same with the P-SB7(except thanks to Gary, now have one), very
cool that if fits in a shirt pocket, with rechargable batteries, and it
works, "they" will use what we use. Kinda torques my jaws though that no one
out there thinks I merit even asking a question of.  If ya don't like me, at
least seek out another ham operator, preferably an extra class. The box is
frikkin' radio, I don't have a lock on it, and hack radios are a good thing.
I don't care what others make and sell. What I say about the RT is based on
what I was told about it at Rolling Hills Asylum, by the maker of the
device, and even that was pitifully little information, like most that see
me, they assume I can't possibly understand the concept of the technology
involved.

 The main criticism with the box is that it picks up broadcast bits, well
that's the function of the box, to provide bits of sounds--speech, music and
noise--for the purpose of creating a raw audio, a material that the entities
can use to form their voices. Radio just happens to be a very convenient
source of raw audio. It works, these sound bits are reorganized in real time
to create meaningful, often interactive voices. They address us by name,
give their names, answer questions, and give other information. The main
limitation is taking it out in the boondocks where there are no radio
staions. So some previously established EVP expert out there doesn't like
it--too bad so sad!  I won't jump through hoops to make it "more
acceptable", besides, it's way, way, way out of my hands.

 In this field, say the wrong thing........
