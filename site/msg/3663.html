Exactly right, Frank. But corporate hackles are easily raised and  
company brands are precious to them and very easily tainted. Look at  
the aftermath of the Tylenol poisonings of the late 1970s--people  
associated death with the product, despite the fact that someone  
completely unassociated tampered with the drugs. For years, Johnson  
and Johnson has been fighting against a rumor started by a competitor  
that their CEO was a member of the Church of Satan--they even went so  
far as to drop their 100-year-old company logo because it had a moon  
and stars in it, just to try to stop the Satanism rumor. My concern  
was that someone had written Radio Shack Corp and told them that some  
devil worshipers or some such where using the company's products to  
promote witchcraft or anti-Christian sentiment or whatever.
