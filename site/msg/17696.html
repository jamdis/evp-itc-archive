To answer your question to me, yes I suppose pareidolia could apply to reading, if the text was blurry or water-damaged or broken up or something (like radio sounds), and someone was trying to figure out what it said.  In those cases, different people could get different things, and not all could be correct.

Also, you mentioned "it seems to evaporate when one tries to pin it down."  To me, pin-it-down means to test it well, under good controlled conditions, to greatly reduce the possibility of coincidence and subjective interpretation.  So, if "it seems to evaporate when one tries to pin it down", then no wonder people don't accept the messages and information others call 'validated'.
