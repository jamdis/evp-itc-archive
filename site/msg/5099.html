I fully agree with Bruce's comment about not leaving a Box running 
without supplying input or asking questions. After I build each 
MiniBox, my original practice was to leave it running overnight as a 
burn-in to insure that if there was an early failure, it would 
happen before the device was shipped. After a while I noticed the 
Boxes would begin cursing or calling me by name repeatedly. By then 
I realized that anytime the Box is activated, entities will attempt 
to communicate within a minute or so. They seem to sense that 
a 'portal' is open so crowd around and try to get the operators 
attention.

I have since changed my procedures and have limited my tests to 
power-up and then alignment, and indicate verbally to 
whoever/whatever is present that this is a test and I am not trying 
to communicate, but to just insure that the device is working 
properly before sending it to its new owner. The incidence of bad 
language has totally disappeared and the Box is actually remarkably 
quiet during this process. As I final test I do a very short test so 
confirm that communications are being made, then thank the entity 
and sign off.

As I have stressed repeatedly, this device, whether a MiniBox, 
Frank's Box, RS Hack, Joe's Box, or whatever, it NOT a toy and 
should be treated with respect. Until more is known, sessions should 
be handled with care and documented properly. I am attempting to 
gather and rewrite my operations guidelines and welcome input from 
everyone. These guidelines are device-independent. I want to insure 
that all persons who read them are using the device properly and 
with the correct attitude.
