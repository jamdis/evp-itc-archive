>  You are right-if you have to do too much enhancing to hear something,
> then you just can't call it EVP.
>
> But that really doesn't mean that there isn't anything there, because there
> could be, but it isn't good enough to be considered.
>
> You can tweak and play with a recording as long as you want, so long as you
> always keep the original in pristine condition for comparison. When I go to
> process my audio, upon opening the track, I immediately save it to another
> name so that I am sure not to accidentally save over the original.
>
> If you have tweaked the audio to the point that it is completely
> unrecognizable from the original, I would say that may be too far.
>
> "Are we just making ourselves believe there is something there??"
>
> Unfortunately I think alot of the times we are. It is called pareidolia, or
> matrixing. It is a natural functioning of our brains to pick out familiar
> patterns out of randomness. Like faces in the clouds and sounds in white
> noise. This make our job of deciphering what is authentic EVP and what is
> just matrixing very difficult. You can't even trust yourself entirely.
>
> Now before Frank kicks my butt on that, I need to state the disclaimer that
> this is only if we wish our evidence to stand up to scientific scrutiny. I
> do know that there is some value to gut feelings, but gut feelings won't get
> you anywhere in the scientific world.
