Well, I got my first BS answers on my last session.  I asked how many entities I was speaking to, and I got two different answers, both repeated twice:  1138 and 14.  Later on, I asked for them to say my mother's name.  The answer in quick clear succession:  Beverley, Stephanie, Jodie.  None of which, needless to say, are my mother's name.  Nor could they have been ANYBODY'S mother's name (at least no one I would expect to talk to), as those are modern names.

I also asked the main guy, Steve, if he knew he was dead, just to see what he'd say.  The answer was a very loud, clear, "Duh!"  Which, if I'm not mistaken, is not the type of language an 88-year-old would use, am I right?  So I'm thinking this Steve is not who I believe he is.  

I did get some evidence on the side of dead relatives, however.  My mom asked, "Is there anyone there who knows me, can you say my name?"  First I hear "Steve," then my mom's name (Sally), and then, very clearly, "Third.  Boy.  Red.  Love.  You.  Red.  Red."  My mom's cousin's nickname was Red, and we weren't entirely sure of this until we looked it up afterward, but he was indeed the third boy.  

I also heard someone saying, all in one quick, short breath, "Get away from me."  This was directly after Red.  Don't know what that was about, but it's the first negative thing I've heard.  Well, earlier in the session, there was something that sounded like "Death Rep" three times in a row.  That was in response to the question, "Are we speaking to Steve, yes or no?"

So, I think during my next session I will try Frank's approach.  I will ask that they all speak together and identify themselves, and then I'll just let them go at it.
