Hi Laura.  I would recommend you buy BOTH of the old 12-470's, as those units are very 
hard to come by.  I'm sure someone would be happy to buy the second one from you, and 
you can make use of the first.  If you don't like it, you can sell it too. 

I've not tried the 20-125 or the 12-820, so someone else will have to give you feedback 
about how they all compare.  The one that really stands out (in not a good way) from the 
other hackables is the new 12-469.  It has an annoying click-click-clicking, which some 
can easily tune out and others cannot.  I have it, as well as the old version 12-469, and old 
12-470.

For manual radio sweep (turning the dial back and forth yourself), I like to use the Duracell 
KP028.  It comes in a 2-pack for around $30, including shipping from places like Amazon 
and eBay.  You might be able to find them in a local store.  I got mine from Costco.  Not 
sure if they have those in your area or not.  I've had better results with this unit than I have 
any of the shack hacks, though I still find the hacks fun to play with.  

Others feel they have had better results with the hacks as compared to manual sweep.  Not 
sure if it has to do with the model of radio they are using or not.  I have tried multiple 
manual sweep units, and that is how I came to like the Duracell as my favorite so far.  I 
also like the Durapro emergency radio, but the thumb dial gives me blisters!

I guess it's just like digital recorders ... one person can get great EVP with a certain make 
and model, while another person will get nothing with it.  LOL - for me, the Olympus I 
have seems to be like that.  I've never had much luck with it.
