Thanks for the info, Bruce.  I guess because I use such a wide variety of digital recorders, 
techniques, software, and settings, what I'm looking for is a way to catalog the session 
clips along with the details.  This is not counting the MiniBox, 12-469, 12-470, either of 
my manual sweep radios, or EVP Maker.  So, like yourself, I too have much more than "one 
box" to keep track of.   I also have a variety of cameras/settings used for ITC images.  LOL 
- I'm in definite need of something "fancy."  ;0)

If I can recall what the file is named, I can use the "spotlight" feature on my MAC to find it, 
though often the content of  a single file is much longer than I could possible name it.  For 
example, my recent results with EVP Maker/SpeakJet Allophones.  I do jot some things 
down in a Mead notebook, but even that can be a chore to go through when I'm wanting to 
find information that is older.

For example, without electronic organization, I can pull up a file and recall that I used 
Diamond Cut Live6/Forensics software, but if I can't refer to some kind of notes, there is 
no way I can recall what the specific settings were unless the session was recent and still 
fresh in my mind (and/or the notes easily found.)

What I'm wanting is some kind of simple database.  It's looking more and more like I'm just 
going to have to buy the software and create it.  If I do, I'll be sure to share the template 
with others who might also be interested.
