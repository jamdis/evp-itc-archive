I think the key is to make sure there is a CONTINIOUS background sound for the entities to work with, and not just the "blips" of sound emmited from the radio. The resonator works at that by filling in the spaces between the radio words--sort of spreading or blending the blips together somewhat.  I also include a white/random noise source with the radio in the resonator pipe.  That seems to help even more (I dont think its really necessary though)  You can see a pic of my Tom's Tube set up in the tom's photos folder in the photo section. (the pipe is a white PVC 24-25" long 2" inside diameter)

Cutting the hole(s) for the radio and noise source(if you have one) seems to be the hardest part of it.  I just held the bottom of the radio against the pipe and marked it with a sharpee.  and drilled some holes with a drill--then filing or cutting away until a hole big enough to accomidate the radio SORTA TIGHT is finally made. Right now I simplu lay the mike end of the voice recorder just inside one of the open ends of the pipe. 

Before I cut the holes, or even thought of mounting the components IN the pipe I simply lay the pipe in front of the radio speaker on my table.  Not very transportable that way, but that worked too. 

A radio shack "nature sounds" unit, "sleep machine" or Avon Spafinder will serve as a good noise source, as like the radio, the speaker is on the "bottom end" that can be inserted into the pipe, leaving the controls accessable on the outside of the pipe. 

My latest improvement is a 2 inch PVC 90degree elbow on one end of the pipe to direct the sound towards me so I can hear it better in real time.  No glue is necessary. it can be swiveled for best listening.

Good luck.
